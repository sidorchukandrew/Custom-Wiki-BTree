      <p>The <b>National Institute of Nursing Research</b> (<b>NINR</b>), as part of the <a href="/wiki/U.S._National_Institutes_of_Health" class="mw-redirect" title="U.S. National Institutes of Health">U.S. National Institutes of Health</a>, supports clinical and basic research to establish a <a href="/wiki/Evidence-based_medicine" title="Evidence-based medicine">scientific basis</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Nurse" class="mw-redirect" title="Nurse">nursing</a> care of individuals across the life span—from management of <a href="/wiki/Patient" title="Patient">patients</a> during illness and recovery, to the reduction of risks for disease and disability, and the promotion of <a href="/wiki/Self_care" class="mw-redirect" title="Self care">healthy lifestyles</a>. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Institute_of_Nursing_Research&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Federal involvement in <a href="/wiki/Nursing_research" title="Nursing research">nursing research</a> can be traced back to 1946, with the establishment of the Division of Nursing within the Office of the <a href="/wiki/Surgeon_General_of_the_United_States" title="Surgeon General of the United States">Surgeon General</a>, Public Health Service. </p>
      <p>In 1955, the first extramural nursing research program was established in the Research Grants and Fellowship Branch of the Division of Nursing Resources, Bureau of Medical Services. At this same time, the <a href="/wiki/National_Institutes_of_Health" title="National Institutes of Health">National Institutes of Health</a> (NIH) established the Nursing Research Study Section within the Division of Research Grants to conduct scientific review of the growing volume of applications in this area. </p>
      <p>In 1960, <a href="/wiki/Public_health_nursing" title="Public health nursing">public health nursing</a> services were consolidated to form a new Division of Nursing at what is now called the <a href="/wiki/Health_Resources_and_Services_Administration" title="Health Resources and Services Administration">Health Resources and Services Administration</a> (HRSA) - the agency responsible for clinical training in the health care professions. The initial and continuing goal of federal support was to build a foundation for nursing research. During this time, many academic institutions established <a href="/wiki/Predoctoral_fellow" title="Predoctoral fellow">predoctoral</a> and <a href="/wiki/Postdoctoral_research" class="mw-redirect" title="Postdoctoral research">postdoctoral</a> <a href="/wiki/Fellow" title="Fellow">fellowship</a> programs to train independent nurse investigators. Nursing research programs were also funded and research information was exchanged across the country. </p>
      <p>The impetus for establishing the National Institute of Nursing Research (NINR) came from the findings of two federal studies. A 1983 report by the <a href="/wiki/Institute_of_Medicine" class="mw-redirect" title="Institute of Medicine">Institute of Medicine</a> recommended that nursing research be included in the mainstream of <a href="/wiki/Medical_research" title="Medical research">biomedical</a> and <a href="/wiki/Behavioural_sciences" title="Behavioural sciences">behavioral</a> science, and a 1984 NIH Task Force study found nursing research activities to be relevant to the NIH mission. These findings resulted in legislative action that established the National Center for Nursing Research (NCNR) at NIH in April 1986. The Center later became an NIH Institute with the NIH <a href="/w/index.php?title=Revitalization_Act_of_1993&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Revitalization Act of 1993 (page does not exist)">Revitalization Act of 1993</a> and a Federal Register notice on June 14 of that year- both actions formally changing the NCNR to the NINR.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> </p>
