   <h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Christian state</h1>

      <p>A <b>Christian state</b> is a country that recognizes a form of <a href="/wiki/Christianity" title="Christianity">Christianity</a> as its official religion and often has a <b>state church</b> (also called an <b>established church</b>),<sup id="cite_ref-Backhouse2011_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Backhouse2011-1">[1]</a></sup> which is a <a href="/wiki/Christian_denomination" title="Christian denomination">Christian denomination</a> that supports the government and is supported by the government.<sup id="cite_ref-Eberle2013_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Eberle2013-2">[2]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Historically, the nations of <a href="/wiki/Armenia" title="Armenia">Armenia</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-MilmanMurdock1887_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MilmanMurdock1887-3">[3]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Ethiopian_Empire" title="Ethiopian Empire">Abyssinia</a> (<a href="/wiki/Ethiopia" title="Ethiopia">Ethiopia</a><a href="/wiki/Eritrea" title="Eritrea">*</a>) ,<sup id="cite_ref-ChingJarzombek2010_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ChingJarzombek2010-4">[4]</a></sup> as well as the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Empire" title="Roman Empire">Roman Empire</a> and <a href="/wiki/Byzantine_Empire" title="Byzantine Empire">Byzantine Empire</a> declared themselves as Christian states.<sup id="cite_ref-Ashby2010_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ashby2010-5">[5]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Frucht2004_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Frucht2004-6">[6]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Today, several nations officially identify themselves as Christian states or have state churches, including <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">Argentina</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-Encyclopædia_Britannica-Argentina_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Encyclopædia_Britannica-Argentina-7">[7]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Costa_Rica" title="Costa Rica">Costa Rica</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-YakobsonRubinstein2009_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-YakobsonRubinstein2009-8">[8]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Denmark" title="Denmark">Denmark</a> (incl. <a href="/wiki/Greenland" title="Greenland">Greenland</a>),<sup id="cite_ref-Shadid1995_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Shadid1995-9">[9]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-Joppke2013_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Joppke2013-10">[10]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Faroe_Islands" title="Faroe Islands">Faroe Islands</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-StatisticsFaroe_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-StatisticsFaroe-11">[11]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(country)" title="Georgia (country)">Georgia</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><a href="/wiki/Greece" title="Greece">Greece</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-Jiang2012_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Jiang2012-13">[13]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Iceland" title="Iceland">Iceland</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-Melton2005_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Melton2005-14">[14]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Liechtenstein" title="Liechtenstein">Liechtenstein</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-Fox2008_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Fox2008-15">[15]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Malta" title="Malta">Malta</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-legal-malta_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-legal-malta-16">[16]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Monaco" title="Monaco">Monaco</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-MonacoReligion_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MonacoReligion-17">[17]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Samoa" title="Samoa">Samoa</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-Wyeth2017_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wyeth2017-18">[18]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Tonga" title="Tonga">Tonga</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-Fodor1986_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Fodor1986-19">[19]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Tuvalu" title="Tuvalu">Tuvalu</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-Temperman2010_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Temperman2010-20">[20]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Vatican_City" title="Vatican City">Vatican City</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-Erasmus2010_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Erasmus2010-21">[21]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Zambia" title="Zambia">Zambia</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Jenkins2011_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Jenkins2011-22">[22]</a></sup> A Christian state stands in contrast to a <a href="/wiki/Secular_state" title="Secular state">secular state</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-Boer2012_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Boer2012-23">[23]</a></sup> an <a href="/wiki/Atheist_state" class="mw-redirect" title="Atheist state">atheist state</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-MarxMcLellan2000_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MarxMcLellan2000-24">[24]</a></sup> or another religious state, such as a <a href="/wiki/Jewish_state" title="Jewish state">Jewish state</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-Burns1996_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Burns1996-25">[25]</a></sup> or an <a href="/wiki/Islamic_state" title="Islamic state">Islamic state</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Sjoberg2006_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Sjoberg2006-26">[26]</a></sup> Though many Christian states have turned secular and adopted the <a href="/wiki/Separation_of_Church_and_state" class="mw-redirect" title="Separation of Church and state">separation of Church and state</a>, church organizations still have much influence in the institutions of these nations, including hospitals and schools with government funding. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Christian_state&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>By 301 AD, the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Armenia_(antiquity)" title="Kingdom of Armenia (antiquity)">Kingdom of Armenia</a> became the first state to declare <a href="/wiki/Early_Christianity" title="Early Christianity">Christianity as its official religion</a> following the conversion of the Royal House of the <a href="/wiki/Arsacids" class="mw-redirect" title="Arsacids">Arsacids</a> in Armenia. The Armenian Apostolic Church is the world's oldest national church. Later, in AD 380, three Roman emperors issued the <a href="/wiki/Edict_of_Thessalonica" title="Edict of Thessalonica">Edict of Thessalonica</a> (<i>Cunctos populos</i>), making the Roman Empire a Christian state,<sup id="cite_ref-Ashby2010_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ashby2010-5">[5]</a></sup> and establishing <a href="/wiki/Nicene_Christianity" title="Nicene Christianity">Nicene Christianity</a>, in the form of its <a href="/wiki/State_church_of_the_Roman_Empire" title="State church of the Roman Empire">State Church</a>, as its official religion.<sup id="cite_ref-IsmaelIsmael2015_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IsmaelIsmael2015-27">[27]</a></sup> </p> 
      <div class="thumb tleft">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;">
        <a href="/wiki/File:Justinian555AD.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9b/Justinian555AD.png/300px-Justinian555AD.png" decoding="async" width="300" height="163" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9b/Justinian555AD.png/450px-Justinian555AD.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9b/Justinian555AD.png/600px-Justinian555AD.png 2x" data-file-width="2111" data-file-height="1144"></a> 
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a href="/wiki/File:Justinian555AD.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a>
         </div>The boundaries of the Byzantine Empire under 
         <a href="/wiki/Justinian_the_Great" class="mw-redirect" title="Justinian the Great">Justinian the Great</a>
        </div>
       </div>
      </div> 
      <p>After the fall of the Western Roman Empire in the late 5th century, the "<a href="/wiki/Byzantine_Empire" title="Byzantine Empire">Byzantine Empire</a>" under the emperor <a href="/wiki/Justinian" class="mw-redirect" title="Justinian">Justinian</a> (reigned 527-565), became the world's predominant Christian state, based on Roman law, Greek culture, and the Greek language."<sup id="cite_ref-Frucht2004_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Frucht2004-6">[6]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Spielvogel2013_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Spielvogel2013-28">[28]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Truxillo2008_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Truxillo2008-29">[29]</a></sup> In this Christian state, in which nearly all of its subjects upheld faith in Jesus, an "enormous amount of artistic talent was poured into the construction of churches, church ceremonies, and church decoration".<sup id="cite_ref-Spielvogel2013_28-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Spielvogel2013-28">[28]</a></sup> John Binns describes this era, writing that:<sup id="cite_ref-Binns2002_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Binns2002-30">[30]</a></sup> </p> 
      <style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r886047036">.mw-parser-output .templatequote{overflow:hidden;margin:1em 0;padding:0 40px}.mw-parser-output .templatequote .templatequotecite{line-height:1.5em;text-align:left;padding-left:1.6em;margin-top:0}</style>
      <blockquote class="templatequote">
       <p>A new stage in the history of the Church began when not just localised communities but nations became Christian. The stage is associated with the conversion of Constantine and the beginnings of a Christian Empire, but the Byzantine Emperor was not the first ruler to lead his people into Christianity, thus setting up the first Christian state. That honour traditionally goes to the church of Armenia.<sup id="cite_ref-Binns2002_30-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Binns2002-30">[30]</a></sup> </p>
      </blockquote> 
      <p>As a Christian state, Armenia "embraced Christianity as the religion of the King, the nobles, and the people".<sup id="cite_ref-MilmanMurdock1887_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MilmanMurdock1887-3">[3]</a></sup> In AD 326, according to official tradition of the Georgian Orthodox Church, following the <a href="/wiki/Conversion_to_Christianity" title="Conversion to Christianity">conversion</a> of <a href="/wiki/Mirian_III_of_Iberia" title="Mirian III of Iberia">Mirian</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nana_of_Iberia" title="Nana of Iberia">Nana</a>, the country of <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(country)" title="Georgia (country)">Georgia</a> became a Christian state, the Emperor Constantine the Great sending clerics for baptising people. In the 4th century AD, in the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Aksum" title="Kingdom of Aksum">Kingdom of Aksum</a>, after <a href="/wiki/Ezana_of_Axum" title="Ezana of Axum">Ezana</a>'s conversion to the faith, this empire also became a Christian state.<sup id="cite_ref-ChingJarzombek2010_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ChingJarzombek2010-4">[4]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-StantonRamsamy2012_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-StantonRamsamy2012-31">[31]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Modern_era">Modern era</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Christian_state&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Modern era">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Argentina">Argentina</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Christian_state&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Argentina">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The Constitution guarantees <a href="/wiki/Freedom_of_religion" title="Freedom of religion">freedom of religion</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEConstitution_of_Argentinaarts._14,_20_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEConstitution_of_Argentinaarts._14,_20-32">[32]</a></sup> Although it enforces neither an official nor a state faith,<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEFayt1985347Bidart_Campos200553_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEFayt1985347Bidart_Campos200553-33">[33]</a></sup> it gives <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholicism" class="mw-redirect" title="Roman Catholicism">Roman Catholicism</a> a preferential status.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEConstitution_of_Argentinaart._2_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEConstitution_of_Argentinaart._2-34">[34]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-irfr1_36-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-irfr1-36">[36]</a></sup> </p> 
      <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r886047036">
      <blockquote class="templatequote">
       <p>The Federal Government supports the Roman Catholic Apostolic religion.</p>
       <div class="templatequotecite">
        — 
        <cite>Section II of <a href="/wiki/Constitution_of_Argentina" title="Constitution of Argentina">Constitution of Argentina</a></cite>
       </div> 
      </blockquote> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Costa_Rica">Costa Rica</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Christian_state&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Costa Rica">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The constitution of <a href="/wiki/Costa_Rica" title="Costa Rica">Costa Rica</a> states that "The Roman Catholic and Apostolic Religion is the religion of the State".<sup id="cite_ref-YakobsonRubinstein2009_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-YakobsonRubinstein2009-8">[8]</a></sup> As such, Catholic Christian <a href="/wiki/Holy_day_of_obligation" title="Holy day of obligation">holy days</a> are recognized by the government and "public schools provide <a href="/wiki/Religious_education" title="Religious education">religious education</a>", although parents are able to opt-out their children if they choose to do so.<sup id="cite_ref-Merriman2009_37-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Merriman2009-37">[37]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Denmark">Denmark</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Christian_state&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Denmark">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

      <p>As early as the 11th century AD, "Denmark was considered to be a Christian state",<sup id="cite_ref-WarburgChristoffersen2013_38-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WarburgChristoffersen2013-38">[38]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> with the <a href="/wiki/Church_of_Denmark" title="Church of Denmark">Church of Denmark</a>, a member of the <a href="/wiki/Lutheran_World_Federation" title="Lutheran World Federation">Lutheran World Federation</a>, being the state church.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> Prof. Wasif Shadid, of <a href="/wiki/Leiden_University" title="Leiden University">Leiden University</a>, writes that: </p>

       <p>The Lutheran established church is a department of the state. Church affairs are governed by a central government ministry, while clergy are government employees. The registration of births, deaths and marriages falls under this ministry of church affairs, and normally speaking the local Lutheran pastor is also the official registrar.<sup id="cite_ref-Shadid1995_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Shadid1995-9">[9]</a></sup> </p>

      <p>82.1% of the population of Denmark are members of the <a href="/wiki/Lutheran" class="mw-redirect" title="Lutheran">Lutheran</a> Church of Denmark, which is "officially headed by the queen of Denmark".<sup id="cite_ref-JuergensmeyerRoof2011_41-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-JuergensmeyerRoof2011-41">[41]</a></sup> Furthermore, clergy "in the Church of Denmark are civil servants employed by the Ministry of Ecclesiastical Affairs" and the "economic base of the Church of Denmark is state-collected church taxes combined with a direct state subsidiary (12%), which symbolically covers the expenses of the Church of Denmark to run the civil registration and the burial system for all citizens."<sup id="cite_ref-JuergensmeyerRoof2011_41-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-JuergensmeyerRoof2011-41">[41]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="England">England</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Christian_state&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: England">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

       <p>Since the <a href="/wiki/Act_of_Settlement_1701" title="Act of Settlement 1701">1701 Act of Establishment</a>, England's official state church has been the <a href="/wiki/Church_of_England" title="Church of England">Church of England</a>, the monarch being its <a href="/wiki/Supreme_Governor_of_the_Church_of_England" title="Supreme Governor of the Church of England">supreme governor</a> and '<a href="/wiki/Defender_of_the_faith" class="mw-redirect" title="Defender of the faith">defender of the faith</a>'.       <p>The <a href="/wiki/Church_of_the_Faroe_Islands" title="Church of the Faroe Islands">Church of the Faroe Islands</a> is the state church of <a href="/wiki/Faroe_Islands" title="Faroe Islands">Faroe Islands</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-StatisticsFaroe_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-StatisticsFaroe-11">[11]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Georgia">Georgia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Christian_state&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Georgia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p><a href="/wiki/Georgia_(country)" title="Georgia (country)">Georgia</a> is one of the oldest Christian states. Article 8 of Georgian Constitution and <a href="/wiki/Concordat_of_2002" title="Concordat of 2002">Concordat of 2002</a> grants <a href="/wiki/Georgian_Orthodox_Church" title="Georgian Orthodox Church">Georgian Orthodox Church</a> special privileges, which includes legal immunity to <a href="/wiki/Patriarch_of_Georgia" class="mw-redirect" title="Patriarch of Georgia">Patriarch of Georgia</a>. Orthodox Church is the most trusted institution in the country.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup> and its head, Patriarch <a href="/wiki/Ilia_II_of_Georgia" title="Ilia II of Georgia">Ilia II</a>-most trusted person.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup> "Fertility changes of this magnitude are not extremely common; many governments would kill for the ability to engineer this kind of policy. But Georgia’s case is unique: comparatively homogenous societies where one highly-respected religious leader has the social capital to induce these kinds of changes are extremely rare. The United States has never had that kind of social homogeneity, nor will it ever." <sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup> Due to this, Georgia is one of the rare exceptions in Eastern Europe, where fertility rates are above replacement level of 2,1.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup> </p>

      <p><a href="/wiki/Greece" title="Greece">Greece</a> is a Christian state,<sup id="cite_ref-Jiang2012_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Jiang2012-13">[13]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-EnyediMadeley2004_51-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EnyediMadeley2004-51">[51]</a></sup> with the <a href="/wiki/Greek_Orthodox_Church" title="Greek Orthodox Church">Greek Orthodox Church</a> playing "a dominant role in the life of the country".<sup id="cite_ref-Meyendorff1981_52-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Meyendorff1981-52">[52]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Greenland">Greenland</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Christian_state&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Greenland">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Norway">Norway</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Christian_state&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Norway">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:242px;">
        <a href="/wiki/File:Norwegian_Bible.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8b/Norwegian_Bible.jpg/240px-Norwegian_Bible.jpg" decoding="async" width="240" height="180" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8b/Norwegian_Bible.jpg/360px-Norwegian_Bible.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8b/Norwegian_Bible.jpg/480px-Norwegian_Bible.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2272" data-file-height="1704"></a> 
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a href="/wiki/File:Norwegian_Bible.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a>
         </div>The 
         <a href="/wiki/Bible" title="Bible">Bible</a> in the 
         <a href="/wiki/Norwegian_language" title="Norwegian language">Norwegian language</a>
        </div>
       </div>
      </div> 
      <p><a href="/wiki/Cole_Durham" title="Cole Durham">Cole Durham</a> and Tore Sam Lindholm, writing in 2013, stated that "For a period of one thousand years Norway has been a kingdom with a Christian <i>state church</i>" and that a decree went out in 1739 ordering that "Elementary schooling for all Norwegian children became mandatory, so that all Norwegians should be able to read the Bible and the <a href="/wiki/Luther%27s_Large_Catechism" title="Luther's Large Catechism">Lutheran Catechism</a> firsthand."<sup id="cite_ref-DurhamLindholm2013_57-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DurhamLindholm2013-57">[57]</a></sup> The modern <a href="/wiki/Constitution_of_Norway" title="Constitution of Norway">Constitution of Norway</a> stipulates that "<a href="/wiki/Church_of_Norway" title="Church of Norway">The Church of Norway</a>, an Evangelical-Lutheran church, will remain the Established Church of Norway and will as such be supported by the State."<sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup> As such, the "Norwegian constitution decrees that <a href="/wiki/Lutheranism" title="Lutheranism">Lutheranism</a> is the official religion of the State and that the King is the supreme temporal head of the Church."<sup id="cite_ref-EriksenAfrikainstitutet2000_59-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EriksenAfrikainstitutet2000-59">[59]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Singh2008_60-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Singh2008-60">[60]</a></sup> The administration of the Church "is shared between the Ministry for Church, Education and Research centrally and municipal authorities locally",<sup id="cite_ref-EriksenAfrikainstitutet2000_59-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EriksenAfrikainstitutet2000-59">[59]</a></sup> and the Church of Norway "depends on state and local taxes".<sup id="cite_ref-Fahlbusch2003_61-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Fahlbusch2003-61">[61]</a></sup> The Church of Norway is responsible for the "maintenance of church buildings and cemeteries".<sup id="cite_ref-62" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-62">[62]</a></sup> John T. Flint writes that "Over 90 percent of the population are married by state church clergymen, have their children <a href="/wiki/Lutheran_sacraments#Baptism" title="Lutheran sacraments">baptized</a> and <a href="/wiki/Confirmation_(Lutheran_Church)" title="Confirmation (Lutheran Church)">confirmed</a>, and finally are <a href="/wiki/Christian_burial" title="Christian burial">buried</a> with a church service."<sup id="cite_ref-Flint1957_63-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Flint1957-63">[63]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Samoa">Samoa</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Christian_state&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Samoa">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p><a href="/wiki/Samoa" title="Samoa">Samoa</a> became a Christian state in 2017. Article 1 of the Samoan Constitution states that “Samoa is a Christian nation founded of God the Father, the Son and the Holy Spirit”.<sup id="cite_ref-Wyeth2017_18-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wyeth2017-18">[18]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tonga">Tonga</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Christian_state&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Tonga">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p><a href="/wiki/Tonga" title="Tonga">Tonga</a> became a Christian state under <a href="/wiki/George_Tupou_I" title="George Tupou I">George Tupou I</a> in the 19th century,<sup id="cite_ref-Fodor1986_19-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Fodor1986-19">[19]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Oliver1989_64-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Oliver1989-64">[64]</a></sup> with the <a href="/wiki/Free_Wesleyan_Church" title="Free Wesleyan Church">Free Wesleyan Church</a>, a member of the <a href="/wiki/World_Methodist_Council" title="World Methodist Council">World Methodist Council</a>, being established as the country's state Church.<sup id="cite_ref-Bell2005_65-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bell2005-65">[65]</a></sup> Under the rule of George I, there was established a "rigorous constitutional clause regulating observation of the <a href="/wiki/Sabbath_in_Christianity" title="Sabbath in Christianity">Sabbath</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-Fodor1986_19-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Fodor1986-19">[19]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tuvalu">Tuvalu</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Christian_state&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Tuvalu">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The <a href="/wiki/Church_of_Tuvalu" title="Church of Tuvalu">Church of Tuvalu</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Reformed_Church" class="mw-redirect" title="Reformed Church">Reformed Church</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Congregationalist" class="mw-redirect" title="Congregationalist">Congregationalist</a> tradition, is the state church of <a href="/wiki/Tuvalu" title="Tuvalu">Tuvalu</a> and was established as such in 1991.<sup id="cite_ref-Ferrari2015_66-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ferrari2015-66">[66]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Constitution_of_Tuvalu" title="Constitution of Tuvalu">Constitution of Tuvalu</a> identifies Tuvalu as "an independent State based on Christian principles".<sup id="cite_ref-Temperman2010_20-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Temperman2010-20">[20]</a></sup> </p>

      <p><a href="/wiki/Vatican_City" title="Vatican City">Vatican City</a> is a Christian state, in which the "<a href="/wiki/Pope" title="Pope">Pope</a> is <i>ex officio</i> simultaneously leader of the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Church" class="mw-redirect" title="Roman Catholic Church">Roman Catholic Church</a> as well as Head of State and Head of the Government of the State of the Vatican City; he also possesses (<i>de jure</i>) absolute authority over the legislative, executive and judicial branches."<sup id="cite_ref-Erasmus2010_21-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Erasmus2010-21">[21]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Zambia">Zambia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Christian_state&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: Zambia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>Jeroen Temperman, a professor of international law at <a href="/wiki/Erasmus_University_Rotterdam" title="Erasmus University Rotterdam">Erasmus University Rotterdam</a> writes that: </p> 
      <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r886047036">
      <blockquote class="templatequote">
       <p><a href="/wiki/Zambia" title="Zambia">Zambia</a> is officially a Christian state as well, though the legal ramifications clearly do not compare to the latter state. The Preamble of the <a href="/wiki/Constitution_of_Zambia" title="Constitution of Zambia">Constitution of Zambia</a> establishes Zambia as a Christian state without specifying "Christian" denominationally. It simply proclaims: "We, the people of Zambia...declare the Republic a Christian nation..." As far as state practice is concerned, it may be pointed out that the Government maintains relations with the Zambian Council of Churches and requires Christianity to be taught in the public school curriculum.<sup id="cite_ref-Rotterdam2010_67-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Rotterdam2010-67">[67]</a></sup> </p>
      </blockquote> 
      <p>After "Zambia declared itself a Christian nation in 1991", "the nation's vice president urged citizens to 'have a <a href="/wiki/Christian_worldview" title="Christian worldview">Christian orientation</a> in all fields, at all levels'."<sup id="cite_ref-Jenkins2011_22-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Jenkins2011-22">[22]</a></sup> </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="National_church">National church</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Christian_state&amp;action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: National church">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>A number of countries have a <a href="/wiki/National_church" title="National church">national church</a> which is not Established (as the official religion of the nation), but is nonetheless recognised under civil law as being the country's acknowledged religious denomination. Whilst these are not Christian states, the official Christian national church is likely to have certain residual state functions in relation to state occasions and ceremonial. Examples include Scotland (<a href="/wiki/Church_of_Scotland" title="Church of Scotland">Church of Scotland</a>) and Sweden (<a href="/wiki/Church_of_Sweden" title="Church of Sweden">Church of Sweden</a>). A national church typically has a monopoly on official state recognition, although unusually <a href="/wiki/Finland" title="Finland">Finland</a> has two national churches (the <a href="/wiki/Evangelical_Lutheran_Church_of_Finland" title="Evangelical Lutheran Church of Finland">Evangelical Lutheran Church of Finland</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Finnish_Orthodox_Church" title="Finnish Orthodox Church">Finnish Orthodox Church</a>), both recognised under civil law as joint official churches of the nation. </p>
