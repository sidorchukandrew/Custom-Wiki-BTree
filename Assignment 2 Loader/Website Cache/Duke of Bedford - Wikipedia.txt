      <p><b>Duke of Bedford</b> (named after <a href="/wiki/Bedford" title="Bedford">Bedford</a>, <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>) is a title that has been created six times (for five distinct people) in the <a href="/wiki/Peerage_of_England" title="Peerage of England">Peerage of England</a>. The first and second creations came in 1414 in favour of <a href="/wiki/Henry_IV_of_England" title="Henry IV of England">Henry IV's</a> third son, <a href="/wiki/John,_Duke_of_Bedford" class="mw-redirect" title="John, Duke of Bedford">John</a>, who later served as regent of France. He was made <b>Earl of Kendal</b> at the same time and was made <b>Earl of Richmond</b> later the same year. The titles became extinct on his death in 1435. The third creation came in 1470 in favour of <a href="/wiki/George_Neville,_1st_Duke_of_Bedford" title="George Neville, 1st Duke of Bedford">George Neville</a>, nephew of <a href="/wiki/Richard_Neville,_Earl_of_Warwick" class="mw-redirect" title="Richard Neville, Earl of Warwick">Warwick the Kingmaker</a>. He was deprived of the title by Act of Parliament in 1478. The fourth creation came 1478 in favour of <a href="/wiki/George_Plantagenet,_Duke_of_Bedford" class="mw-redirect" title="George Plantagenet, Duke of Bedford">George</a>, the third son of <a href="/wiki/Edward_IV_of_England" title="Edward IV of England">Edward IV</a>. He died the following year at the age of two. The fifth creation came in 1485 in favour of <a href="/wiki/Jasper_Tudor" title="Jasper Tudor">Jasper Tudor</a>, half-brother of <a href="/wiki/Henry_VI_of_England" title="Henry VI of England">Henry VI</a> and uncle of <a href="/wiki/Henry_VII_of_England" title="Henry VII of England">Henry VII</a>. He had already been created <b>Earl of Pembroke</b> in 1452. However, as he was a Lancastrian, his title was forfeited between 1461 and 1485 during the predominance of the <a href="/wiki/House_of_York" title="House of York">House of York</a>. He regained the earldom in 1485 when his nephew Henry VII came to the throne and was elevated to the dukedom the same year. He had no legitimate children and the titles became extinct on his death in 1495. </p>
      <p>The Russell family currently holds the titles of Earl and Duke of Bedford. John Russell, a close adviser of <a href="/wiki/Henry_VIII_of_England" title="Henry VIII of England">Henry VIII</a> and <a href="/wiki/Edward_VI_of_England" title="Edward VI of England">Edward VI</a>, was granted the title of <b>Earl of Bedford</b> in 1551, and his descendant William, 5th Earl, was created Duke following the <a href="/wiki/Glorious_Revolution" title="Glorious Revolution">Glorious Revolution</a>. </p>
      <p>The subsidiary titles of the Duke of Bedford, all in the <a href="/wiki/Peerage_of_England" title="Peerage of England">Peerage of England</a>, are <b>Marquess of Tavistock</b> (created 1694), <b>Earl of Bedford</b> (1550), <b>Baron Russell</b>, of Cheneys (1539), <b>Baron Russell of Thornhaugh</b> in the County of <a href="/wiki/Northamptonshire" title="Northamptonshire">Northampton</a> (1603), and <b>Baron Howland</b>, of <a href="/wiki/Streatham" title="Streatham">Streatham</a> in the County of <a href="/wiki/Surrey" title="Surrey">Surrey</a> (1695) (and possibly the <a href="/wiki/Barony_of_Bedford" title="Barony of Bedford">Barony of Bedford</a>, which was merged into it in 1138, 1366 or 1414). The <a href="/wiki/Courtesy_title" title="Courtesy title">courtesy title</a> of the Duke of Bedford's eldest son and heir is <i>Marquess of <a href="/wiki/Tavistock,_Devon" class="mw-redirect" title="Tavistock, Devon">Tavistock</a></i>. </p>
      <p>Every Duke from the <a href="/wiki/Francis_Russell,_5th_Duke_of_Bedford" title="Francis Russell, 5th Duke of Bedford">5th Duke</a> onwards is descended from <a href="/wiki/Charles_II_of_England" title="Charles II of England">Charles II of England</a>. The family seat is <a href="/wiki/Woburn_Abbey" title="Woburn Abbey">Woburn Abbey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bedfordshire" title="Bedfordshire">Bedfordshire</a>. The private mausoleum and chapel of the Russell Family and the Dukes of Bedford is at <a href="/wiki/St_Michael%27s,_Chenies" title="St Michael's, Chenies">St. Michael’s Church</a> in <a href="/wiki/Chenies" title="Chenies">Chenies</a>, <a href="/wiki/Buckinghamshire" title="Buckinghamshire">Buckinghamshire</a> (<a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.british-history.ac.uk/sites/default/files/publications/pubid-1302/images/fig83.jpg">photo</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The family owns <a href="/wiki/The_Bedford_Estate" class="mw-redirect" title="The Bedford Estate">The Bedford Estate</a> in central London. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Coat_of_arms">Coat of arms</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Duke_of_Bedford&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Coat of arms">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
        <a href="/wiki/File:Bookplate-William_Duke_of_Bedford.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/70/Bookplate-William_Duke_of_Bedford.jpg/220px-Bookplate-William_Duke_of_Bedford.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="227" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/70/Bookplate-William_Duke_of_Bedford.jpg/330px-Bookplate-William_Duke_of_Bedford.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/70/Bookplate-William_Duke_of_Bedford.jpg/440px-Bookplate-William_Duke_of_Bedford.jpg 2x" data-file-width="994" data-file-height="1024"></a> 
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">

         <a href="/wiki/Bookplate" title="Bookplate">Bookplate</a> showing the

        </div>
       </div>

      <p>The heraldic blazon for the coat of arms of the Russell dukedom is: <i>Argent, a lion rampant gules; on a chief sable, three escallops of the first</i>. </p>
      <p>The arms show a claim to be descended from the medieval lord Hugh de Rozel, which has been debunked, especially by <a href="/wiki/J._Horace_Round" title="J. Horace Round">J. Horace Round</a> in his essay <i>The Rozels of Bedford".<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> The family tree on the website of <a href="/wiki/Woburn_Abbey" title="Woburn Abbey">Woburn Abbey</a> only refers to the descent from the provable Stephen Russell in 1394.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></i> </p>
      <p>The chief from these arms is present in the arms of the modern <a href="/wiki/Coat_of_arms_of_the_London_Borough_of_Camden" title="Coat of arms of the London Borough of Camden">coat of arms of the London Borough of Camden</a>, because the dukes of Bedford used to own land in the present borough. </p>
