      <p>The <b><i lang="hr" title="Croatian language text">Lista za Rijeku</i> - <i lang="it" title="Italian language text">Lista per Fiume</i></b> (meaning <i>List for <a href="/wiki/Rijeka" title="Rijeka">Rijeka</a></i> in <a href="/wiki/Croatian_language" title="Croatian language">Croatian</a> and <a href="/wiki/Italian_language" title="Italian language">Italian</a>) is a regional political party with strong local-patriotic, autonomous and progressive-liberal tendencies, based in the city of Rijeka, <a href="/wiki/Croatia" title="Croatia">Croatia</a> (known as Fiume in Italian). Founded in 2006, the party is active primarily within the confines of the city of Rijeka and the boroughs surrounding it (the so-called Rijeka ring). Its main aim is to achieve increased authority and development for the regional and local government through the retention of 70% of the locally collected taxes, which currently go to the <a href="/wiki/Croatian_government" class="mw-redirect" title="Croatian government">central government</a> in <a href="/wiki/Zagreb" title="Zagreb">Zagreb</a>. The party also advocates a high level of <a href="/wiki/Self-governance" title="Self-governance">autonomy</a> for city of Rijeka and the surrounding areas. </p>
      <p>The current party president is <a href="/w/index.php?title=Danko_%C5%A0vorini%C4%87&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Danko Švorinić (page does not exist)">Danko Švorinić</a>, and its governing body is a 9-member presidency council. </p>
      <p>The party has two members in the Rijeka city council (receiving 6% of the vote in the <a href="/wiki/2013_Croatian_local_elections" title="2013 Croatian local elections">local elections of 2013</a>) and 8 elected councilors in city district councils. </p>
      <p>Since 2010, Lista za Rijeku - Lista per Fiume is a full member of the <a href="/wiki/European_Free_Alliance" title="European Free Alliance">European Free Alliance</a>. </p>
