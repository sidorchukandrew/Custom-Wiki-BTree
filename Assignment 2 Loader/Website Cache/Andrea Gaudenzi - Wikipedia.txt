      <p><b>Andrea Gaudenzi</b> (<small>Italian pronunciation:&nbsp;</small><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Italian" title="Help:IPA/Italian">[anˈdrɛːa ɡauˈdɛntsi]</a></span>); born 30 July 1973 is a former <a href="/wiki/Tennis_player" class="mw-redirect" title="Tennis player">tennis player</a> from Italy, who turned professional in 1990 after becoming Junior World Champion winning both Roland Garros and US Open. He reached his career-high <a href="/wiki/Association_of_Tennis_Professionals" title="Association of Tennis Professionals">ATP</a> singles ranking in February 1995, when he became World No. 18. </p>
      <p>Gaudenzi was born in <a href="/wiki/Faenza" title="Faenza">Faenza</a>, <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>, has victories over <a href="/wiki/Roger_Federer" title="Roger Federer">Roger Federer</a> in 2002 Rome, <a href="/wiki/Pete_Sampras" title="Pete Sampras">Pete Sampras</a> in the first round of the <a href="/wiki/2002_French_Open_-_Men%27s_Singles" class="mw-redirect" title="2002 French Open - Men's Singles">2002 French Open</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jim_Courier" title="Jim Courier">Jim Courier</a> in the US Open 1994 and <a href="/wiki/Goran_Ivanisevic" class="mw-redirect" title="Goran Ivanisevic">Goran Ivanisevic</a>, <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Muster" title="Thomas Muster">Thomas Muster</a>, <a href="/wiki/Michael_Stich" title="Michael Stich">Michael Stich</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yevgeny_Kafelnikov" title="Yevgeny Kafelnikov">Yevgeny Kafelnikov</a>. A right-hander, he represented his native country at the <a href="/wiki/1996_Summer_Olympics" title="1996 Summer Olympics">1996 Summer Olympics</a> in <a href="/wiki/Atlanta" title="Atlanta">Atlanta</a>, where he was defeated in the third round by the eventual winner, <a href="/wiki/Andre_Agassi" title="Andre Agassi">Andre Agassi</a> and reached the <a href="/wiki/Davis_Cup" title="Davis Cup">Davis Cup</a> Final in 1998, semifinals in 1995 and 1996, playing both singles and doubles. Great clay court player, very consistent on this surface. He won 3 ATP Tour Titles and 6 Finals. Also reached the semi-final in the Monte Carlo Master Series in 1995, losing to Thomas Muster. </p>
      <p>Gaudenzi graduated in law from <a href="/wiki/University_of_Bologna" title="University of Bologna">University of Bologna</a> and obtained an MBA with Honours at <a href="/wiki/International_University_of_Monaco" title="International University of Monaco">IUM</a>. </p>
      <p>He currently serves as <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.atpmedia.tv/">ATP Media</a> board member and is a Board Member and Chief Revenues Officer at <a href="/wiki/Musixmatch" title="Musixmatch">Musixmatch</a>. </p>
      <p><a href="/wiki/Musixmatch" title="Musixmatch">Musixmatch</a> is the global leading consumer and music data company based in London, San Francisco and Bologna. Raised a total of €15M from Venture Capitalist (United Ventures and P101).The company focuses on building the world's largest music Metadata and Lyrics Database using latest AI &amp; Machine Learning technology (NLP). <a href="/wiki/Musixmatch" title="Musixmatch">Musixmatch</a> has more than 70 million users worldwide throughout all consumer platforms and has global clients like Apple Music, Amazon Music, Instagram, Facebook, Google Search and Spotify with the goal of enhancing the consumer music experience. </p>
      <p>He also co-founded and was CMO at <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.soldo.com">Soldo</a>, the multi-user expense account featuring Mastercard cards, intuitive admin and effortless reporting. Streamlines the entire business expense cycle from spending to reconciliation. Soldo's innovation and talented team has attracted in excess of $83 million, in both Seed, <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.businessinsider.com/banking-startup-soldo-raises-11million-from-accel-for-expansion-2017-6?r=US&amp;IR=T">Series A</a> and <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://techcrunch.com/2019/07/07/soldo-series-b/">Series B</a> funding, led by Accel Partners, Battery Ventures and Dawn Capital. </p>
      <p>Andrea is now an Entrepreneurial and driven C-Suite Executive, with 20 years of industry-leading expertise. Launching and managing numerous companies within the media and entertainment industry. Facilitator and builder of world-class management and product development teams, with a specialty in both start-up and scaled growth stages. Proven fundraising and networking skills, building fruitful partnerships with hundreds of clients and delivering multimillion-dollar bottom-line growth. </p>
      <p>Andrea lives in London, has three boys who play tennis in the LTA circuit. </p>
