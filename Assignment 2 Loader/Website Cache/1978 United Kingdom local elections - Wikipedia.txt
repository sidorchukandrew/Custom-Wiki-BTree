      <p>Local elections were held in the United Kingdom in 1978. Elections took place in the London boroughs and metropolitan districts.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The main opposition Conservative Party gained 275 seats, bringing their number of councillors to 12,645. They gained Oldham and Havering from no overall control, and Ealing, Hillingdon, Wandsworth and Sandwell from Labour. </p>
      <p>The governing Labour Party lost 461 seats, leaving them with 6,644 councillors. In addition to their losses to the Conservatives they also lost South Tyneside, Hammersmith and Fulham and Wolverhampton to no overall control. </p>
      <p>The Liberal Party lost 163 seats in total, leaving them with 923 councillors. </p>

      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="District_councils">District councils</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1978_United_Kingdom_local_elections&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: District councils">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>In 44 districts one third of the council was up for election. </p>
      <p>These were the first councils which had passed a resolution under section 7 (4) (b) of the <a href="/wiki/Local_Government_Act_1972" title="Local Government Act 1972">Local Government Act 1972</a>, requesting a system of elections by thirds. They could do so because they had had their new ward boundaries introduced at the 1976 elections. </p>
