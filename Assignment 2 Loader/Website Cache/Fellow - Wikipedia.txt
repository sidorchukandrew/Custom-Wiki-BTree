      <p>A <b>fellow</b> is a member of an <a href="/wiki/Academy" title="Academy">academy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Learned_society" title="Learned society">learned society</a> or group of learned people (a <b>fellowship</b>) which works together in pursuing mutual knowledge or practice.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> There are many different kinds of fellowships which are awarded for different reasons in academia and industry. These often indicate a different level of <a href="/wiki/Scholarship" title="Scholarship">scholarship</a>. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education_and_academia">Education and academia<span id="Education"></span><span id="Academia"></span></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fellow&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Education and academia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>In education and academia there are several kinds of fellowships, awarded for different reasons. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Teaching_fellowships">Teaching fellowships</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fellow&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Teaching fellowships">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       Main article: 
       <a href="/wiki/Teaching_fellow" title="Teaching fellow">Teaching fellow</a>
      </div> 
      <p>The title of (senior) teaching fellow is used to denote an academic teaching position at a university or similar institution and is roughly equivalent to the title of (senior) lecturer. The title <i>(senior) fellow</i> can also be bestowed to an academic member of staff upon retirement who continues to be affiliated to a university in the United Kingdom. </p>
      <p>The term teaching fellow or <a href="/wiki/Teaching_assistant" title="Teaching assistant">teaching assistant</a> is used, in the United States and United Kingdom, in secondary school, high school and middle school setting for students or adults that assist a teacher with one or more classes.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Medical_fellowships">Medical fellowships</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fellow&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Medical fellowships">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       Main article: 
       <a href="/wiki/Fellowship_(medicine)" title="Fellowship (medicine)">Fellowship (medicine)</a>
      </div> 
      <p>In US medical institutions, a fellow refers to someone who has completed residency training (e.g. in internal medicine, pediatrics, general surgery, etc.) and is currently in a 1 to 3 year subspecialty training program (e.g. cardiology, pediatric nephrology, transplant surgery, etc.). </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Research_fellowships">Research fellowships</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fellow&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Research fellowships">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="As_an_academic_position">As an academic position<span id="Position"></span></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fellow&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: As an academic position">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       Main article: 
       <a href="/wiki/Research_fellow" title="Research fellow">Research fellow</a>
      </div> 
      <p>The title of research fellow may be used to denote an <a href="/wiki/Academic_position" class="mw-redirect" title="Academic position">academic position</a> at a university or a similar institution; it is roughly equivalent to the title of <a href="/wiki/Lecturer" title="Lecturer">lecturer</a> in the teaching career pathway.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> </p> 
      <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="As_a_financial_grant">As a financial grant<span id="Funding"></span></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fellow&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: As a financial grant">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4> 
      <p>Research fellow may also refer to the recipient of academic financial grant or <a href="/wiki/Scholarship" title="Scholarship">scholarship</a>. For example, in Germany, institutions such as the <a href="/wiki/Alexander_von_Humboldt_Foundation" title="Alexander von Humboldt Foundation">Alexander von Humboldt Foundation</a> offer research fellowship for postdoctoral research and refer to the holder as research fellows, while the award holder may formally hold a specific <a href="/wiki/Academic_ranks" class="mw-redirect" title="Academic ranks">academic title</a> at their home institution (e.g., <i><a href="/wiki/Privatdozent" title="Privatdozent">Privatdozent</a></i>).<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>These are often shortened to the name of the programme or organization, e.g. <a href="/wiki/Dorothy_Hodgkin" title="Dorothy Hodgkin">Dorothy Hodgkin</a> Fellow rather than Dorothy Hodgkin Research Fellow, except where this might cause confusion with another fellowship, (e.g. <a href="/wiki/Royal_Society_University_Research_Fellowship" title="Royal Society University Research Fellowship">Royal Society University Research Fellowship</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-urf_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-urf-6">[6]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-urfs_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-urfs-7">[7]</a></sup>) </p>
      <p>In the context of <a href="/wiki/Graduate_school" title="Graduate school">graduate school</a> in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> and <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>, a fellow is a recipient of a <a href="/wiki/Postgraduate" class="mw-redirect" title="Postgraduate">postgraduate</a> fellowship. Examples include the <a href="/wiki/NSF-GRF" class="mw-redirect" title="NSF-GRF">NSF Graduate Research Fellowship</a>, the National Defense Science and Engineering Graduate Fellowship, the <a href="/wiki/Guggenheim_Fellowship" title="Guggenheim Fellowship">Guggenheim Fellowship</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Harold_Wallace_Rosenthal" title="Harold Wallace Rosenthal">Rosenthal Fellowship</a>, <a href="/wiki/Woodrow_Wilson_Teaching_Fellowship" title="Woodrow Wilson Teaching Fellowship">Woodrow Wilson Teaching Fellowship</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Presidential_Management_Fellowship" class="mw-redirect" title="Presidential Management Fellowship">Presidential Management Fellowship</a>. It is granted to prospective or current students, on the basis of their academic or research achievements. </p>
      <p>In the UK, research fellowships are awarded to support <a href="/wiki/Postdoctoral_research" class="mw-redirect" title="Postdoctoral research">postdoctoral researchers</a> such as those funded by the <a href="/wiki/Wellcome_Trust" title="Wellcome Trust">Wellcome Trust</a><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> and the <a href="/wiki/Biotechnology_and_Biological_Sciences_Research_Council" title="Biotechnology and Biological Sciences Research Council">Biotechnology and Biological Sciences Research Council (BBSRC)</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> At <a href="/wiki/ETH_Zurich" title="ETH Zurich">ETH Zurich</a>, postdoctoral fellowships support incoming researchers.<sup id="cite_ref-eth_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-eth-10">[10]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/MacArthur_Fellows_Program" title="MacArthur Fellows Program">MacArthur Fellows Program</a> (aka "genius grant") as prestigious research fellowship awarded in the United States. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Fellowships_as_a_training_program">Fellowships as a training program<span id="Training"></span></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fellow&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Fellowships as a training program">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>Fellowships may involve a short placement for capacity building,<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> e.g., to get more experience in government, such as the <a href="/wiki/American_Association_for_the_Advancement_of_Science" title="American Association for the Advancement of Science">American Association for the Advancement of Science</a>'s fellowships<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> and the <a href="/wiki/American_Academy_of_Arts_and_Sciences#Fellowship_programs" title="American Academy of Arts and Sciences">American Academy of Arts and Sciences#Fellowship programs</a>. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Fellowships_as_a_special_membership_grade">Fellowships as a special membership grade</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fellow&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Fellowships as a special membership grade">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       Main article: 
       <a href="/wiki/Learned_society_fellow" class="mw-redirect" title="Learned society fellow">Learned society fellow</a>
      </div> 
      <p>Fellows are often the highest grade of membership of many <a href="/wiki/Professional_association" title="Professional association">professional associations</a> or <a href="/wiki/Learned_society" title="Learned society">learned societies</a>, for example, the <a href="/wiki/Chartered_Institute_of_Arbitrators" title="Chartered Institute of Arbitrators">Chartered Institute of Arbitrators</a> or <a href="/wiki/Royal_College_of_Surgeons" title="Royal College of Surgeons">Royal College of Surgeons</a>. Lower grades are referred to as members (who typically share voting rights with the fellows), or associates (who may or may not, depending on whether "associate" status is a form of full membership). Additional grades of membership exist in, for example, the <a href="/wiki/Institute_of_Electrical_and_Electronics_Engineers#Membership_grades" title="Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers">IEEE</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Association_for_Computing_Machinery#Membership_grades" title="Association for Computing Machinery">ACM</a>. </p>
      <p>Fellowships of this type can be awarded as a <a href="/wiki/Title_of_honor" title="Title of honor">title of honor</a> in their own right, e.g. the <a href="/wiki/Fellow_of_the_Royal_Society" title="Fellow of the Royal Society">Fellowship of the Royal Society (FRS)</a>. Exclusive <a href="/wiki/Learned_society" title="Learned society">learned societies</a> such as the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Society" title="Royal Society">Royal Society</a> have Fellow as the only grade of membership. </p>
      <p>Appointment as an honorary fellow in a learned or professional society can be either to honour exceptional achievement and/or service within the professional domain of the awarding body or to honour contributions related to the domain from someone who is professionally outside it. Membership of the awarding body may or may not be a requirement. </p>
      <p>How a fellowship is awarded varies for each society, but may typically involve some or all of these: </p> 
      <ul>
       <li>A qualifying period in a lower grade</li> 
       <li>Passing a series of examinations</li> 
       <li>Nomination by two existing fellows who know the applicant professionally</li> 
       <li>Evidence of continued formal training post-qualification</li> 
       <li>Evidence of substantial achievement in the subject area</li> 
       <li>Submission of a thesis or portfolio of works which will be examined</li> 
       <li>Election by a vote of the fellowship</li>
      </ul> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="In_ancient_universities">In ancient universities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fellow&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: In ancient universities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p><span id="university_fellows_dons"></span> At the <a href="/wiki/Ancient_universities" class="mw-redirect" title="Ancient universities">ancient universities</a> of the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Oxford" title="University of Oxford">University of Oxford</a>, the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Cambridge" title="University of Cambridge">University of Cambridge</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Trinity_College,_Dublin" class="mw-redirect" title="Trinity College, Dublin">Trinity College, Dublin</a>, members of the teaching staff typically have two affiliations: one as a reader, lecturer, or other <a href="/wiki/Academic_rank" class="mw-redirect" title="Academic rank">academic rank</a> within a department of the university, as at other universities, and a second affiliation as a fellow of one of the <a href="/wiki/Residential_college" title="Residential college">colleges</a> of the university. The fellows, sometimes referred to as <a href="/wiki/University_don" class="mw-redirect" title="University don">University dons</a>, form the <a href="/wiki/Governance" title="Governance">governing body</a> of the college. They may elect a council to handle day-to-day management. All fellows are entitled to certain privileges within their colleges, which may include dining at <a href="/wiki/High_Table" class="mw-redirect" title="High Table">High Table</a> (free of charge) and possibly the right to a room in college (free of charge). </p>
      <p>At Cambridge, retired academics may remain fellows<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>. At Oxford, however, a Governing Body fellow would normally be elected a <i>fellow emeritus</i> and would leave the Governing Body upon his or her retirement.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Distinguished old members of the college, or its benefactors and friends, might also be elected 'Honorary Fellow', normally for life; but beyond limited <a href="/wiki/Dining_rights" title="Dining rights">dining rights</a> this is merely an honour. Most Oxford colleges have 'Fellows by Special Election' or 'Supernumerary Fellows', who may be members of the teaching staff, but not necessarily members of the Governing Body. </p>
      <p>Some senior administrators of a college such as <a href="/wiki/Bursar" title="Bursar">bursars</a> are made fellows, and thereby become members of the governing body, because of their importance to the running of a college<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="In_the_U.S.">In the U.S.</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fellow&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: In the U.S.">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       See also: 
       <a href="/wiki/President_and_Fellows_of_Harvard_College" title="President and Fellows of Harvard College">President and Fellows of Harvard College</a>
      </div> 
      <p>At <a href="/wiki/Harvard_University" title="Harvard University">Harvard University</a> and some other universities in the United States, "fellows" are members of the <a href="/wiki/Board_of_Governors" class="mw-redirect" title="Board of Governors">Board of Trustees</a> who hold administrative positions as non-executive <a href="/wiki/Trustee" title="Trustee">trustee</a> rather than academics<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Industry_and_corporate_fellows">Industry and corporate fellows</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fellow&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Industry and corporate fellows">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       See also: 
       <a href="/wiki/IBM_Fellow" title="IBM Fellow">IBM Fellow</a> and 
       <a href="/wiki/ICL_Fellows" title="ICL Fellows">ICL Fellows</a>
      </div> 
      <p>In industries intensive in <a href="/wiki/Research_and_development" title="Research and development">research and development</a> (R&amp;D), companies may appoint a very small number of top senior researchers as corporate or technical Fellows, either in <a href="/wiki/Science" title="Science">Science</a> or in <a href="/wiki/Engineering" title="Engineering">Engineering</a>. These are internationally recognized R&amp;D leaders who are among the best in the world in their respective fields<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup>. </p>
      <p>Fellow is the most senior rank or title one can achieve in a scientific or engineering <a href="/wiki/Career" title="Career">career</a>, though fellows often also hold business titles such as <a href="/wiki/Vice_President" class="mw-redirect" title="Vice President">Vice President</a><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> or <a href="/wiki/Chief_Technology_Officer" class="mw-redirect" title="Chief Technology Officer">Chief Technology Officer</a><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup>. </p>
      <p>Notable examples of science and engineering fellows in R&amp;D-intensive organizations include <a href="/wiki/IBM_Fellow" title="IBM Fellow">IBM Fellows</a>, <a href="/wiki/Category:Microsoft_technical_fellows" title="Category:Microsoft technical fellows">Microsoft Fellows</a>, <a href="/wiki/Boeing_Technical_Fellowship" title="Boeing Technical Fellowship">Boeing Fellows</a> and <a href="/wiki/ICL_Fellows" title="ICL Fellows">ICL Fellows</a>. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Nonprofit_and_government_fellowships">Nonprofit and government fellowships</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fellow&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Nonprofit and government fellowships">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The title fellow can be used for participants in a professional development program run by a <a href="/wiki/Nonprofit_organization" title="Nonprofit organization">nonprofit</a> or governmental organization. This type of fellowship is a short term work opportunity (1–2 years)<sup id="cite_ref-Find_Non-Profit_Fellowships_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Find_Non-Profit_Fellowships-21">[21]</a></sup> for <a href="/wiki/Professional" title="Professional">professionals</a> who already possess some level of academic or professional expertise that will serve the nonprofit mission. Fellows are given a <a href="/wiki/Stipend" title="Stipend">stipend</a> as well as professional experience and leadership training. </p>
