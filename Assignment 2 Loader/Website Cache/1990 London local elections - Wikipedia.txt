      <p><b><a href="/wiki/Local_government_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="Local government in the United Kingdom">Local government</a></b> <a href="/wiki/Elections_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="Elections in the United Kingdom">elections</a> took place in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>, and some other parts of the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a> on Thursday 3 May 1990. </p>
      <p>All <a href="/wiki/London_borough" class="mw-redirect" title="London borough">London borough</a> council seats were up for election. The previous Borough elections in London were in 1986.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The elections saw a poor result for the recently formed Liberal Democrats, with the party losing 10% of its vote and 20 council seats. This decline was accompanied by success for the Conservatives, who gained 46 councillors and 1 council, while Labour lost 32 council seats and 1 council. The <a href="/wiki/Green_Party_(UK)" title="Green Party (UK)">Green Party</a>, fresh from winning 15% of the vote in the <a href="/wiki/1989_European_Parliament_election_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="1989 European Parliament election in the United Kingdom">European elections</a> one year previously, achieved a record vote share of 6% but elected no councillors. The party would not exceed this result again until <a href="/wiki/2006_London_local_elections" title="2006 London local elections">2006</a>. </p>
