      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Introduction">Introduction</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Liberalism_by_country&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Introduction">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The definition of liberal party is highly debatable. In the list below, it is defined as a <a href="/wiki/Political_party" title="Political party">political party</a> that adheres to the basic principles of political <a href="/wiki/Liberalism" title="Liberalism">liberalism</a>. This is a broad political current, including <a href="/wiki/Left-wing_politics" title="Left-wing politics">left-wing</a>, <a href="/wiki/Centrism" title="Centrism">centrist</a> and <a href="/wiki/Right-wing_politics" title="Right-wing politics">right-wing</a> elements. All liberal parties emphasise <a href="/wiki/Individual_and_group_rights" title="Individual and group rights">individual rights</a>, but they differ in their opinion on an active role for the state. This list includes parties of different character, ranging from <a href="/wiki/Classical_liberalism" title="Classical liberalism">classical liberalism</a> to <a href="/wiki/Social_liberalism" title="Social liberalism">social liberalism</a>, <a href="/wiki/Conservative_liberalism" title="Conservative liberalism">conservative liberalism</a> to <a href="/wiki/National_liberalism" title="National liberalism">national liberalism</a>. </p>
      <p>Several <a href="/wiki/Conservatism" title="Conservatism">conservative</a> and/or <a href="/wiki/Christian_democracy" title="Christian democracy">Christian-democratic</a> parties, such as the British <a href="/wiki/Conservative_Party_(UK)" title="Conservative Party (UK)">Conservative Party</a>, Germany's <a href="/wiki/Christian_Democratic_Union_of_Germany" title="Christian Democratic Union of Germany">Christian Democratic Union</a> and Spain's <a href="/wiki/People%27s_Party_(Spain)" title="People's Party (Spain)">People's Party</a>, are also considered to be <a href="/wiki/Neoliberalism" title="Neoliberalism">neoliberal</a> leaning or have strong <a href="/wiki/Liberal_conservatism" title="Liberal conservatism">liberal conservative</a> and/or classical liberal factions, whereas some conservative parties, such as Poland's <a href="/wiki/Law_and_Justice" title="Law and Justice">Law and Justice</a> and Hungary's <a href="/wiki/Fidesz" title="Fidesz">Fidesz</a>, while favour more state intervention also support free market/free market solutions. Conversely, some social-democratic parties, such as the British <a href="/wiki/Labour_Party_(UK)" title="Labour Party (UK)">Labour Party</a> and the Italian <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Party_(Italy)" title="Democratic Party (Italy)">Democratic Party</a>, include liberal elements. Social liberalism and <a href="/wiki/Social_conservatism" title="Social conservatism">social conservatism</a> are not mutually exclusive either, in fact some parties espouse socially liberal economic policies, while maintaining more socially conservative or traditionalist views on society: examples of this include Finland's <a href="/wiki/Centre_Party_(Finland)" title="Centre Party (Finland)">Centre Party</a> (see also <a href="/wiki/Nordic_agrarian_parties" title="Nordic agrarian parties">Nordic agrarian parties</a>) and Ireland's <a href="/wiki/Fianna_F%C3%A1il" title="Fianna Fáil">Fianna Fáil</a>, both members of the <a href="/wiki/Alliance_of_Liberals_and_Democrats_for_Europe_Party" title="Alliance of Liberals and Democrats for Europe Party">Alliance of Liberals and Democrats for Europe Party</a> (ALDE Party). In the United States, the two major political forces, the <a href="/wiki/Republican_Party_(United_States)" title="Republican Party (United States)">Republican Party</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Party_(United_States)" title="Democratic Party (United States)">Democratic Party</a>, are to some extent, liberal (see <a href="/wiki/Liberalism_in_the_United_States" title="Liberalism in the United States">Liberalism in the United States</a> and <a href="/wiki/Modern_liberalism_in_the_United_States" title="Modern liberalism in the United States">Modern liberalism in the United States</a>). </p>
      <p>Many liberal parties are members of the <a href="/wiki/Liberal_International" title="Liberal International">Liberal International</a> and/or one of its regional partners, such as the ALDE Party in Europe, the <a href="/wiki/Liberal_Network_for_Latin_America" title="Liberal Network for Latin America">Liberal Network for Latin America</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Council_of_Asian_Liberals_and_Democrats" title="Council of Asian Liberals and Democrats">Council of Asian Liberals and Democrats</a>. Generally, membership in these international organizations is an indication that that party is indeed liberal. However, other international organisations, such as the <a href="/wiki/International_Democrat_Union" title="International Democrat Union">International Democrat Union</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Centrist_Democrat_International" title="Centrist Democrat International">Centrist Democrat International</a>, and regional organisations, such as the <a href="/wiki/European_People%27s_Party" title="European People's Party">European People's Party</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Alliance_of_European_Conservatives_and_Reformists" class="mw-redirect" title="Alliance of European Conservatives and Reformists">Alliance of European Conservatives and Reformists</a>, the <a href="/wiki/European_Democratic_Party" title="European Democratic Party">European Democratic Party</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Christian_Democrat_Organization_of_America" title="Christian Democrat Organization of America">Christian Democrat Organization of America</a>, also have liberal or liberal leaning parties as significant proportions of their membership. </p>
      <p>Not all the parties using the "Liberal" or "Freedom" labels are actually liberal. Moreover, some parties, such as the <a href="/wiki/Freedom_Party_of_Austria" title="Freedom Party of Austria">Freedom Party of Austria</a>, were originally liberal, but have since tilted toward a <a href="/wiki/Populism" title="Populism">populist</a> direction and abandoned most of the tenets of liberalism. Finally, some parties, such as the United States Republican Party, Australia's <a href="/wiki/Liberal_Party_of_Australia" title="Liberal Party of Australia">Liberal Party</a> or Norway's <a href="/wiki/Progress_Party_(Norway)" title="Progress Party (Norway)">Progress Party</a> are liberal mainly from an economic point of view (see <a href="/wiki/Economic_liberalism" title="Economic liberalism">economic liberalism</a>, <a href="/wiki/Libertarianism" title="Libertarianism">libertarianism</a> and <a href="/wiki/Right-libertarianism" title="Right-libertarianism">right-libertarianism</a>). </p>

      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Africa">Africa</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Liberalism_by_country&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Africa">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>Liberalism is a relatively new current for <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>. Traditionally it only existed more or less in <a href="/wiki/Liberalism_in_Egypt" title="Liberalism in Egypt">Egypt</a>, <a href="/wiki/Liberalism_in_Senegal" title="Liberalism in Senegal">Senegal</a> and especially <a href="/wiki/Liberalism_in_South_Africa" title="Liberalism in South Africa">South Africa</a>. </p> 
      <ul>
       <li>There are very few liberal political parties in <a href="/wiki/List_of_political_parties_in_Algeria" title="List of political parties in Algeria">Algeria</a>. <a href="/wiki/Ahd_54" title="Ahd 54">Ahd 54</a> and the Algerian Natural Law Party may be considered liberal. The main <a href="/wiki/Berber_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Berber people">Berber</a> party, the <a href="/wiki/Rally_for_Culture_and_Democracy" title="Rally for Culture and Democracy">Rally for Culture and Democracy</a> (Rassemblement pour la Culture et la Démocratie) could be considered to embrace some liberal values.</li> 
       <li>In <a href="/wiki/List_of_political_parties_in_Angola" title="List of political parties in Angola">Angola</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Liberal_Democratic_Party_(Angola)" title="Liberal Democratic Party (Angola)">Liberal Democratic Party</a> (Partido Liberal Democratico, member <a href="/wiki/Liberal_International" title="Liberal International">LI</a>) is a small liberal party.</li> 
       <li>In <a href="/wiki/List_of_political_parties_in_Benin" title="List of political parties in Benin">Benin</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Rebirth_Party_of_Benin" class="mw-redirect" title="Rebirth Party of Benin">Rebirth Party of Benin</a> (Parti de la renaissance du Bénin), might be considered a liberal party, but its exact profile is not available. The <a href="/wiki/Cowry_Forces_for_an_Emerging_Benin" title="Cowry Forces for an Emerging Benin">Cowry Forces for an Emerging Benin</a> (Forces Cauris pour un Bénin émergent) are a centrist party alliance with liberal elements.</li> 
       <li>In <a href="/wiki/List_of_political_parties_in_Burkina_Faso" title="List of political parties in Burkina Faso">Burkina Faso</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Alliance_for_Democracy_and_Federation" title="Alliance for Democracy and Federation">Alliance for Democracy and Federation</a> (Alliance pour la Démocratie et la Fédération), might be considered a liberal party, but it exact profile is not available.</li> 
       <li>In <a href="/wiki/List_of_political_parties_in_Cape_Verde" title="List of political parties in Cape Verde">Cape Verde</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Movement_for_Democracy_(Cape_Verde)" title="Movement for Democracy (Cape Verde)">Movement for Democracy</a> (Movimento para a Democracia, member <a href="/wiki/Centrist_Democrat_International" title="Centrist Democrat International">CDI</a>), might be considered a liberal party, but its exact profile is not available.</li> 
       <li>In <a href="/wiki/List_of_political_parties_in_C%C3%B4te_d%27Ivoire" class="mw-redirect" title="List of political parties in Côte d'Ivoire">Côte d'Ivoire</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Rally_of_the_Republicans" title="Rally of the Republicans">Rally of the Republicans</a> (Rassemblement des Republicains, member <a href="/wiki/Liberal_International" title="Liberal International">LI</a>, <a href="/wiki/Centrist_Democrat_International" title="Centrist Democrat International">CDI</a>) is the liberal, main government party.</li> 
       <li>In <a href="/wiki/List_of_political_parties_in_Egypt" title="List of political parties in Egypt">Egypt</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Front_Party_(Egypt)" class="mw-redirect" title="Democratic Front Party (Egypt)">Democratic Front Party</a> (Hizb al-Gabha Al-dimocratia) and the <a href="/wiki/New_Delegation_Party" class="mw-redirect" title="New Delegation Party">New Delegation Party</a> (Hizb al-Wafd-al-Jadid) could be considered "liberal parties". The newest liberal party in Egypt is <a href="/wiki/El-Ghad_Party" title="El-Ghad Party">El-Ghad Party</a> (Tomorrow's Party) led by the opposition leader Ayman Nour <a rel="nofollow" class="external autonumber" href="https://web.archive.org/web/20180413164456/http://elghad.org/">[1]</a>. Also, the newly established <a href="/wiki/Free_Egyptians_Party" title="Free Egyptians Party">Free Egyptians Party</a>. <i>See for more information: <a href="/wiki/Liberalism_in_Egypt" title="Liberalism in Egypt">Liberalism in Egypt</a></i>.</li> 
       <li>In <a href="/wiki/List_of_political_parties_in_Equatorial_Guinea" title="List of political parties in Equatorial Guinea">Equatorial Guinea</a>, the <a href="/wiki/National_Democratic_Union_of_Equatorial_Guinea" title="National Democratic Union of Equatorial Guinea">National Democratic Union of Equatorial Guinea</a> (Unión Democratica Nacional de Guinea Ecuatorial, member <a href="/wiki/Liberal_International" title="Liberal International">LI</a>) claims to be a liberal party.</li> 
       <li>In <a href="/wiki/List_of_political_parties_in_Gambia" class="mw-redirect" title="List of political parties in Gambia">Gambia</a>, the <a href="/wiki/United_Democratic_Party_(Gambia)" class="mw-redirect" title="United Democratic Party (Gambia)">United Democratic Party</a>, might be considered a liberal party, but it exact profile is not available.</li> 
       <li>In <a href="/wiki/List_of_political_parties_in_Ghana" title="List of political parties in Ghana">Ghana</a>, the <a href="/wiki/New_Patriotic_Party" title="New Patriotic Party">New Patriotic Party</a> is a <a href="/wiki/Right-wing_politics" title="Right-wing politics">right of center</a> liberal party that is unclear about its international affiliations.</li>

      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Americas">The Americas</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Liberalism_by_country&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: The Americas">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>In many Latin American countries, liberalism and radicalism have been associated with generally left-of-center political movements such as Colombia's Liberal Party, historically concerned mostly with effecting government decentralization and regional autonomy (liberals were influential in the total dissolution of at least two defunct countries, the <a href="/wiki/United_Provinces_of_Central_America" class="mw-redirect" title="United Provinces of Central America">United Provinces of Central America</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gran_Colombia" title="Gran Colombia">Gran Colombia</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Separation_of_church_and_state" title="Separation of church and state">separation of church and state</a>. At times, the anti-clerical and secularist stances promoted by Latin American liberals have resulted in limitations on the civil rights of clergy or others associated with the Church (as in Mexico, where law still prohibits priests from public office). Liberalism in North America has a different background. </p> 
      <ul>
       <li>In <a href="/wiki/List_of_political_parties_in_Argentina" title="List of political parties in Argentina">Argentina</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Radical_Civic_Union" title="Radical Civic Union">Radical Civic Union</a> (Unión Cívica Radical) historically was a centrist progressive-liberal party, while nowadays it adheres to the <a href="/wiki/Socialist_International" title="Socialist International">Socialist International</a> and its platform is a combination of liberal and social democratic ideas. The UCR's long-time rivals have been <a href="/wiki/Peronism" title="Peronism">Peronism</a> and the Peronist-inspired <a href="/wiki/Justicialist_Party" title="Justicialist Party">Justicialist Party</a>. <a href="/wiki/Recreate_for_Growth" title="Recreate for Growth">Recreate for Growth</a> (Recrear para el Crecimiento) had been a short lived attempt to form a market liberal party and has observer status in the <a href="/wiki/Liberal_International" title="Liberal International">Liberal International</a>. This party was in alliance with conservative-liberal <a href="/wiki/Republican_Proposal" title="Republican Proposal">Republican Proposal</a> (Propuesta Republicana). Also smaller parties, such as the <a href="/wiki/Union_of_the_Democratic_Centre_(Argentina)" title="Union of the Democratic Centre (Argentina)">Union of the Democratic Centre</a> (Unión del Centro Democrático), the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Progressive_Democratic_Party_(Argentina)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Progressive Democratic Party (Argentina) (page does not exist)">Progressive Democratic Party</a> (Partido Demócrata Progresista), the <a href="/wiki/Liberal_Party_of_Corrientes" title="Liberal Party of Corrientes">Liberal Party of Corrientes</a> (Partido Liberal de Corrientes) and the <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Party_(Mendoza)" title="Democratic Party (Mendoza)">Democratic Party of Mendoza</a> (Partido Demócrata Mendoza), adhere to conservative-liberal principles. On the libertarian side, stands the <a href="/wiki/Liberal_Libertarian_Party" title="Liberal Libertarian Party">Liberal Libertarian Party</a> (Partido Liberal Libertario), whose focus is on free markets and individual rights.</li>

       <li>In the <a href="/wiki/List_of_political_parties_in_the_United_States" title="List of political parties in the United States">United States</a>, the primary use of the term <i>liberal</i> is at some variance with European and worldwide usage. In the United States today, it is most associated with the definition of <a href="/wiki/Modern_liberalism_in_the_United_States" title="Modern liberalism in the United States">modern liberalism</a>, which is a combination of <a href="/wiki/Social_liberalism" title="Social liberalism">social liberalism</a>, <a href="/wiki/Public_welfare" class="mw-redirect" title="Public welfare">public welfare</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Mixed_economy" title="Mixed economy">mixed economy</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-Schlesinger_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Schlesinger-1">[1]</a></sup> which is in contrast to <a href="/wiki/Classical_liberalism" title="Classical liberalism">classical liberalism</a>. In the 19th century it was not a common term in American philosophy or politics, partially because the two main parties were a mixture of <a href="/wiki/Populism" title="Populism">populist</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nationalist" class="mw-redirect" title="Nationalist">nationalist</a> elements. ("Conservatism" was not a common term until the mid-20th century as well.) The <b><a href="/wiki/Democratic_Party_(United_States)" title="Democratic Party (United States)">Democratic Party</a></b> was the party of free trade, low tariffs and laissez-faire entrepreneurialism, while the <b><a href="/wiki/Republican_Party_(United_States)" title="Republican Party (United States)">Republican Party</a></b> advocated national citizenship, transparency and government efforts to stabilize the currency. Liberalism in the United States was primarily defined by the self-proclaimed liberal presidents <a href="/wiki/Woodrow_Wilson" title="Woodrow Wilson">Woodrow Wilson</a> and <a href="/wiki/Franklin_Roosevelt" class="mw-redirect" title="Franklin Roosevelt">Franklin Roosevelt</a>. While the emphasis on mutual collaboration through liberal institutions as an alternative to the threat and use of force remained consistent with international liberalism, United States liberals also claimed that individuals have a right to expect the government to guarantee <a href="/wiki/Social_justice" title="Social justice">social justice</a>. This was in part a consequence of the influence of the ideas of British economist <a href="/wiki/John_Maynard_Keynes" title="John Maynard Keynes">John Maynard Keynes</a> on <a href="/wiki/Franklin_D._Roosevelt" title="Franklin D. Roosevelt">Franklin D. Roosevelt</a>'s <a href="/wiki/New_Deal" title="New Deal">New Deal</a>. The New Deal had the effect of stealing the thunder of social democratic forces and the necessity to prevent social unrest strengthened this development. As the term <i><a href="/wiki/Socialism" title="Socialism">socialism</a></i> was (and is still) commonly perceived as communistic (as in the <a href="/wiki/Ideology_of_the_Communist_Party_of_the_Soviet_Union" title="Ideology of the Communist Party of the Soviet Union">ideology of the USSR</a>), many to the left of center moderated their views, aligning with the New Deal liberals. The Democratic Party is identified as the liberal party within the broader definition of <a href="/wiki/Liberalism" title="Liberalism">liberalism</a> thus putting it in contrast with most other parties listed here. Democrats advocate more social freedoms, <a href="/wiki/Affirmative_action" title="Affirmative action">affirmative action</a>, and a mixed economy (and therefore modern liberalism). The <a href="/wiki/Republican_Party_(United_States)" title="Republican Party (United States)">Republican Party</a> experiences a somewhat fractured economic viewpoint with some members supporting strong free-market and <a href="/wiki/Libertarianism" title="Libertarianism">libertarian</a> views (and therefore <a href="/wiki/Economic_liberalism" title="Economic liberalism">economic liberalism</a>) and others championing pro-business stances, though both sectors typically mix their fiscal views with strong aspects of <a href="/wiki/Social_conservatism" title="Social conservatism">social conservatism</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Libertarian_Party_(United_States)" title="Libertarian Party (United States)">Libertarian Party</a> is the third largest political party in the United States, (though still only getting 1–2% of the vote in congressional elections), and particularly centers itself on free markets and individual liberty, which is more in line with <a href="/wiki/Classical_liberalism" title="Classical liberalism">classical liberalism</a>. <i>(Main article: <a href="/wiki/Liberalism_in_the_United_States" title="Liberalism in the United States">Liberalism in the United States</a> and <a href="/wiki/Modern_liberalism_in_the_United_States" title="Modern liberalism in the United States">Modern liberalism in the United States</a>)</i></li> 
       <li>In <a href="/wiki/List_of_political_parties_in_Uruguay" title="List of political parties in Uruguay">Uruguay</a>, liberalism organized itself in the nineteenth century in the <a href="/wiki/Colorado_Party_(Uruguay)" title="Colorado Party (Uruguay)">Colorado Party</a> (Partido Colorado) nowadays a heterogeneous party, divided in factions ranging from conservative to social-democratic; however, its general profile is more or less liberal. <i>(Main article: <a href="/wiki/Liberalism_in_Uruguay" title="Liberalism in Uruguay">Liberalism in Uruguay</a>).</i></li> 
       <li>In <a href="/wiki/List_of_political_parties_in_Venezuela" title="List of political parties in Venezuela">Venezuela</a>, liberalism was a strong force in the nineteenth and beginning of the twentieth century. Nowadays there are three important classical liberal movements (still no parties): <a href="/wiki/Organization_for_the_Liberal_Democracy_in_Venezuela" title="Organization for the Liberal Democracy in Venezuela">Organization for the Liberal Democracy in Venezuela</a> (Organización por la Democracia Liberal en Venezuela), a classical liberal, pro-capitalism think-tank; Liberal Democratic Movement (<a href="/wiki/Movimiento_Dem%C3%B3crata_Liberal" title="Movimiento Demócrata Liberal">Movimiento Demócrata Liberal</a>) and "<a href="/w/index.php?title=Rumbo_Propio_para_el_Zulia&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Rumbo Propio para el Zulia (page does not exist)">Rumbo Propio para el Zulia</a>" from Maracaibo, Zulia, a classical liberal autonomist movement. They are going to create together a political party in the next years. <i>(Main article: <a href="/wiki/Liberalism_in_Venezuela" class="mw-redirect" title="Liberalism in Venezuela">Liberalism in Venezuela</a>).</i></li>
      </ul> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Asia">Asia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Liberalism_by_country&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Asia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Europe">Europe</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Liberalism_by_country&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Europe">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>At a pan-European level liberalism exists in some form within generally all members of the <a href="/wiki/Alliance_of_Liberals_and_Democrats_for_Europe_Party" title="Alliance of Liberals and Democrats for Europe Party">Alliance of Liberals and Democrats for Europe Party</a> (ALDE), within most members of the <a href="/wiki/European_Democratic_Party" title="European Democratic Party">European Democratic Party</a> (EDP), within many members of the <a href="/wiki/European_People%27s_Party" title="European People's Party">European People's Party</a> (EPP) and some members of the <a href="/wiki/Alliance_of_European_Conservatives_and_Reformists" class="mw-redirect" title="Alliance of European Conservatives and Reformists">Alliance of European Conservatives and Reformists</a> (AECR). </p> 
      <ul>
       <li>In <a href="/wiki/List_of_political_parties_in_Albania" title="List of political parties in Albania">Albania</a>, liberalism is weak. Two parties could be considered to embrace liberal values: the <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Alliance_Party_(Albania)" title="Democratic Alliance Party (Albania)">Democratic Alliance Party</a> (Partia Aleanca Demokratike, member <a href="/wiki/Liberal_International" title="Liberal International">LI</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alliance_of_Liberals_and_Democrats_for_Europe_Party" title="Alliance of Liberals and Democrats for Europe Party">ALDE</a>) and the <a href="/wiki/Unity_for_Human_Rights_Party" title="Unity for Human Rights Party">Unity for Human Rights Party</a> (Partia Bashkimi për të Drejtat e Njeriut), which is the party of the ethnic minorities. <i>(Main article: <a href="/wiki/Liberalism_in_Albania" title="Liberalism in Albania">Liberalism in Albania</a>.)</i></li> 
       <li>In <a href="/wiki/List_of_political_parties_in_Andorra" title="List of political parties in Andorra">Andorra</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Liberal_Party_of_Andorra" title="Liberal Party of Andorra">Liberal Party of Andorra</a> (Partit Liberal d'Andorra, member <a href="/wiki/Liberal_International" title="Liberal International">LI</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alliance_of_Liberals_and_Democrats_for_Europe_Party" title="Alliance of Liberals and Democrats for Europe Party">ALDE</a>) is a centre-right liberal party and currently the second-largest political party by parliamentary representation.</li> 
       <li>In <a href="/wiki/List_of_political_parties_in_Armenia" title="List of political parties in Armenia">Armenia</a>, traditional liberalism made a comeback following the <a href="/wiki/2018_Armenian_revolution" title="2018 Armenian revolution">2018 Velvet Revolution</a>. After the <a href="/wiki/2018_Armenian_parliamentary_election" title="2018 Armenian parliamentary election">2018 Armenian parliamentary election</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bright_Armenia" title="Bright Armenia">Bright Armenia</a>, a liberal political party, became the official opposition in the <a href="/wiki/National_Assembly_(Armenia)" title="National Assembly (Armenia)">National Assembly</a> and is a member of the <a href="/wiki/Alliance_of_Liberals_and_Democrats_for_Europe_Party" title="Alliance of Liberals and Democrats for Europe Party">ALDE</a>. <i>(Main article: <a href="/wiki/Liberalism_in_Armenia" title="Liberalism in Armenia">Liberalism in Armenia</a>.)</i></li> 
       <li>In <a href="/wiki/List_of_political_parties_in_Austria" title="List of political parties in Austria">Austria</a>, liberalism almost disappeared, when the <a href="/wiki/Liberal_Forum" title="Liberal Forum">Liberal Forum</a> (Liberales Forum) became a micro-party before re-entering parliament in 2013 on the electoral list of <a href="/wiki/NEOS_%E2%80%93_The_New_Austria" class="mw-redirect" title="NEOS – The New Austria">NEOS – The New Austria</a>, a new liberal party with which it merged in January 2014, a member of <a href="/wiki/Alliance_of_Liberals_and_Democrats_for_Europe_Party" title="Alliance of Liberals and Democrats for Europe Party">ALDE</a>). Historically the now right-wing populist <a href="/wiki/Freedom_Party_of_Austria" title="Freedom Party of Austria">Freedom Party of Austria</a> was considered to be liberal or national-liberal, and was a member of the Liberal International until 1993. <i>(Main article: <a href="/wiki/Liberalism_in_Austria" title="Liberalism in Austria">Liberalism in Austria</a>.)</i></li> 
       <li>In <a href="/wiki/List_of_political_parties_in_Belarus" title="List of political parties in Belarus">Belarus</a>, one of the main opposition parties is the liberal <a href="/wiki/United_Civil_Party_of_Belarus" class="mw-redirect" title="United Civil Party of Belarus">United Civil Party of Belarus</a> (Abjadnanaja Hramadzianskaja Partyja Biełarusi).</li> 
       <li>The <a href="/wiki/List_of_political_parties_in_Belgium" class="mw-redirect" title="List of political parties in Belgium">Belgian</a> party system is divided by language.        <li>In <a href="/wiki/List_of_political_parties_in_France" title="List of political parties in France">France</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Radical_Party_of_the_Left" title="Radical Party of the Left">Radical Party of the Left</a> (Parti radical de gauche) is a former member of <a href="/wiki/Alliance_of_Liberals_and_Democrats_for_Europe_Party" title="Alliance of Liberals and Democrats for Europe Party">ELDR</a> but not of <a href="/wiki/Liberal_International" title="Liberal International">LI</a>. France had an important liberal tradition, generally associated to <a href="/wiki/Radicalism_(historical)#France" title="Radicalism (historical)">Republicanism</a>, from which the right and the left of the political spectrum were generated. On the right-wing there were the <i>Republicans</i>, which organized themselves in 1901-03 in the moderate-liberal <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Republican_Alliance" title="Democratic Republican Alliance">Democratic Republican Alliance</a> and in the liberal-conservative <a href="/wiki/Republican_Federation_(France)" class="mw-redirect" title="Republican Federation (France)">Republican Federation</a>; on the left-wing the <i>Radicals</i>, which founded the <a href="/wiki/Radical_Party_(France)" title="Radical Party (France)">Republican, Radical and Radical-Socialist Party</a> in 1901. After World War II, the Republicans gathered in the liberal-conservative <a href="/wiki/National_Center_of_Independents_and_Peasants" class="mw-redirect" title="National Center of Independents and Peasants">National Center of Independents and Peasants</a>, from which the conservative-liberal <a href="/wiki/Independent_Republicans" title="Independent Republicans">Independent Republicans</a> seceded in 1962. The original centre-left Radical Party was a declining force in French politics until 1972 when it joined the centre-right, causing the split of Radical-Socialist faction and the foundation of the Radical Party of the Left, closely associated to the <a href="/wiki/Socialist_Party_(France)" title="Socialist Party (France)">Socialist Party</a>. In 1978 both the <a href="/wiki/Republican_Party_(France)" title="Republican Party (France)">Republican Party</a> (successor of the Independent Republicans) and the Radical Party were founding components, alongside with <a href="/wiki/Centre_of_Social_Democrats" title="Centre of Social Democrats">Centre of Social Democrats</a>, of the <a href="/wiki/Union_for_French_Democracy" title="Union for French Democracy">Union for French Democracy (UDF)</a>, an alliance of liberal and Christian-democratic forces. The Republican Party, re-founded as <a href="/wiki/Liberal_Democracy_(France)" title="Liberal Democracy (France)">Liberal Democracy</a> in 1997 and re-shaped as a free-market libertarian party, left UDF in 1998 and merged into the Gaulist <a href="/wiki/Union_for_a_Popular_Movement" title="Union for a Popular Movement">Union for a Popular Movement (UMP)</a>, of which it represented the libertarian wing. Also the Radical Party left UDF in 2002 in order to join UMP, of which it is the main social-liberal component, as an associate party. In some ways we can say that the Republican tradition and the Radical one are now re-composed in UMP. Anyway, the lack of a true liberal and libertarian force in France led to the creation in 2006 of <a href="/wiki/Liberal_Alternative" title="Liberal Alternative">Liberal Alternative</a>, whose chances of success are unclear. In 2017, <a href="/wiki/Emmanuel_Macron" title="Emmanuel Macron">Emmanuel Macron</a> formed the liberal party <a href="/wiki/La_R%C3%A9publique_En_Marche!" title="La République En Marche!">La République En Marche!</a> and won the presidential and the National Assembly elections. <a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Democratic_Movement_(France)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="The Democratic Movement (France) (page does not exist)">The Democratic Movement (France)</a>, the successor of the <a href="/wiki/Union_for_French_Democracy" title="Union for French Democracy">Union for French Democracy</a>, joined forces with <a href="/wiki/La_R%C3%A9publique_En_Marche!" title="La République En Marche!">La République En Marche!</a>. <i>(Main article: <a href="/wiki/Liberalism_and_radicalism_in_France" title="Liberalism and radicalism in France">Liberalism and radicalism in France</a>).</i></li>

       <li>In the <a href="/wiki/List_of_political_parties_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="List of political parties in the United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>, liberalism is now represented mainly by the <a href="/wiki/Liberal_Democrats_(UK)" title="Liberal Democrats (UK)">Liberal Democrats</a> (member <a href="/wiki/Liberal_International" title="Liberal International">LI</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alliance_of_Liberals_and_Democrats_for_Europe_Party" title="Alliance of Liberals and Democrats for Europe Party">ALDE</a>), formed in 1988 from a merger of the historical <a href="/wiki/Liberal_Party_(UK)" title="Liberal Party (UK)">Liberal Party</a> with the <a href="/wiki/Social_Democratic_Party_(UK)" title="Social Democratic Party (UK)">Social Democratic Party</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Liberal_Party_(UK,_1989)" title="Liberal Party (UK, 1989)">Liberal Party</a> was re-founded in 1989, still exists, has several Councillors in England, but at present no parliamentary representation. The Liberal Democrats, which has hundreds of Councillors and 18 <a href="/wiki/Member_of_parliament" title="Member of parliament">Members of Parliament</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> were the junior party in a governing coalition with the <a href="/wiki/Conservative_Party_(UK)" title="Conservative Party (UK)">Conservative Party</a> in 2010-2015. The counterpart of the Liberal Democrats in Northern Ireland is the <a href="/wiki/Alliance_Party_of_Northern_Ireland" title="Alliance Party of Northern Ireland">Alliance Party of Northern Ireland</a> (member <a href="/wiki/Liberal_International" title="Liberal International">LI</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alliance_of_Liberals_and_Democrats_for_Europe_Party" title="Alliance of Liberals and Democrats for Europe Party">ALDE</a>), which was formed in 1970 as a non-ideological cross-community party. <i>(Main article <a href="/wiki/Liberalism_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="Liberalism in the United Kingdom">Liberalism in the United Kingdom</a>)</i></li>
      </ul> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Oceania">Oceania</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Liberalism_by_country&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Oceania">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>Liberalism has a strong tradition in both <a href="/wiki/Liberalism_in_Australia" title="Liberalism in Australia">Australia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Liberalism_in_New_Zealand" class="mw-redirect" title="Liberalism in New Zealand">New Zealand</a>. </p> 
      <ul>
       <li>In <a href="/wiki/List_of_political_parties_in_Australia" title="List of political parties in Australia">Australia</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Liberal_Party_of_Australia" title="Liberal Party of Australia">Liberal Party</a> is a party considered to be <a href="/wiki/Centre-right" class="mw-redirect" title="Centre-right">centre-right</a>. Within the Liberal Party of Australia, there is a fusion of <a href="/wiki/Liberalism" title="Liberalism">liberal</a> and <a href="/wiki/Conservative" class="mw-redirect" title="Conservative">conservative</a> views, bound by an opposition to <a href="/wiki/Socialism" title="Socialism">socialism</a>. Many would argue that this party is a <a href="/wiki/Classical_liberalism" title="Classical liberalism">classical liberal</a> party and that the perception of what liberalism is has changed, not the Liberal Party (which promotes the <a href="/wiki/Free_market" title="Free market">free market</a> approach that liberals worldwide used to promote before the 20th century). Former Australian Prime Minister <a href="/wiki/Malcolm_Turnbull" title="Malcolm Turnbull">Malcolm Turnbull</a>, a member of the Liberal Party, has said that his party is "not a conservative party". The term <i><a href="/wiki/Small-l_liberal" class="mw-redirect" title="Small-l liberal">small-l liberal</a></i> generally refers to someone who champions civil liberties and progressive causes such as <a href="/wiki/Australian_republicanism" class="mw-redirect" title="Australian republicanism">Australian republicanism</a> and <a href="/wiki/Indigenous_Australians#History" title="Indigenous Australians">reconciliation with Indigenous Australians</a>. These views are represented strongly within the broad Liberal Party, as well as in parties such as the <a href="/wiki/Australian_Democrats" title="Australian Democrats">Australian Democrats</a>, which began its life as a group of social-liberals disaffected with the Liberal Party. The <a href="/wiki/Liberal_Democratic_Party_(Australia)" title="Liberal Democratic Party (Australia)">Liberal Democratic Party</a> is a classical liberal and libertarian party. <i>(Main article: <a href="/wiki/Liberalism_in_Australia" title="Liberalism in Australia">Australia</a>).</i></li> 
       <li>In the <a href="/wiki/List_of_political_parties_in_the_Cook_Islands" title="List of political parties in the Cook Islands">Cook Islands</a> the liberal <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Party_(Cook_Islands)" title="Democratic Party (Cook Islands)">Democratic Party</a> is one of the two major parties opposing the nationalist <a href="/wiki/Cook_Islands_Party" title="Cook Islands Party">Cook Islands Party</a>.</li> 
       <li>In <a href="/wiki/List_of_political_parties_in_New_Zealand" title="List of political parties in New Zealand">New Zealand</a>, there is no longer a pure liberal party, as in the past the <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand_Liberal_Party" title="New Zealand Liberal Party">Liberal Party</a> was the first organised political party, and the <a href="/wiki/Liberal_Government_of_New_Zealand" title="Liberal Government of New Zealand">Liberal Government</a> from 1891 to 1912 was responsible for many reforms. Similarly to Australia, this party merged with more conservative and free market forces to form the <a href="/wiki/National_Party_of_New_Zealand" class="mw-redirect" title="National Party of New Zealand">National Party of New Zealand</a>, in order to oppose the advancement of the democratic socialist <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand_Labour_Party" title="New Zealand Labour Party">New Zealand Labour Party</a>. <i>Liberalism</i> nowadays refers to a support for individual liberties and limited government. The term is generally used with a reference to a particular policy area, e.g. "market liberalism" or "social liberalism". Unqualified liberalism is less common; in its extreme form it is known by the American term <i>libertarianism</i>. The left of centre <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand_Democratic_Party" class="mw-redirect" title="New Zealand Democratic Party">New Zealand Democratic Party</a> takes a more or less progressive liberal position in the spectrum, but lost popular support. <a href="/wiki/ACT_New_Zealand" title="ACT New Zealand">ACT New Zealand</a> is a classical liberal or libertarian party. <i>(Main article: <a href="/wiki/Liberalism_in_New_Zealand" class="mw-redirect" title="Liberalism in New Zealand">Liberalism in New Zealand</a>).</i></li> 
       <li>In the <a href="/wiki/List_of_political_parties_in_the_Solomon_Islands" class="mw-redirect" title="List of political parties in the Solomon Islands">Solomon Islands</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Solomon_Islands_Liberal_Party" title="Solomon Islands Liberal Party">Solomon Islands Liberal Party</a> considers itself a liberal party.</li>
      </ul>
