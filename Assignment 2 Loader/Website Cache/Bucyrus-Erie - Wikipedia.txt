      <p><b>Bucyrus-Erie</b> was an American surface and underground mining equipment company. It was founded as <b>Bucyrus Foundry and Manufacturing Company</b> in <a href="/wiki/Bucyrus,_Ohio" title="Bucyrus, Ohio">Bucyrus, Ohio</a> in 1880. Bucyrus moved its headquarters to <a href="/wiki/South_Milwaukee,_Wisconsin" title="South Milwaukee, Wisconsin">South Milwaukee, Wisconsin</a> in 1893. In 1927, Bucyrus merged with the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Erie_Steam_Shovel_Company&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Erie Steam Shovel Company (page does not exist)">Erie Steam Shovel Company</a> to form Bucyrus-Erie. </p>
      <p>Renamed <b>Bucyrus International, Inc.</b> in 1997, it was purchased by <a href="/wiki/Caterpillar_Inc." title="Caterpillar Inc.">Caterpillar Inc.</a> in a US$7.6 billion<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> ($8.6 billion including net debt) transaction that closed on July 8, 2011. At the time of its acquisition, the Bucyrus product line included a range of material removal and material handling products used in both surface and underground mining. </p>

      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1880-1927">1880-1927</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bucyrus-Erie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 1880-1927">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>Bucyrus was an early producer of <a href="/wiki/Steam_shovel" title="Steam shovel">steam shovels</a>, operating from its Bucyrus, Ohio headquarters and manufacturing facility. In 1893, Bucyrus moved its operations to South Milwaukee, Wisconsin.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEBogue1985150_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEBogue1985150-8">[8]</a></sup> </p> 
      <div class="thumb tleft">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
        <a href="/wiki/File:Bucyrus_Pan_American.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/65/Bucyrus_Pan_American.png/220px-Bucyrus_Pan_American.png" decoding="async" width="220" height="120" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/65/Bucyrus_Pan_American.png/330px-Bucyrus_Pan_American.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/65/Bucyrus_Pan_American.png/440px-Bucyrus_Pan_American.png 2x" data-file-width="1511" data-file-height="825"></a> 
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a href="/wiki/File:Bucyrus_Pan_American.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a>
         </div>A Bucyrus steam shovel working in the Panama Canal
        </div>
       </div>
      </div> 
      <p>In 1904 Bucyrus supplied 77 of the 102 <a href="/wiki/Steam_shovel" title="Steam shovel">steam shovels</a> used to dig the <a href="/wiki/Panama_Canal" title="Panama Canal">Panama Canal</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEBucyrustimeline_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEBucyrustimeline-9">[9]</a></sup> These were ninety-five-ton models with five-cubic-yard buckets that could move approximately eight tons of material at once. They were operated by a crew of four. Similar to a <a href="/wiki/Locomotive" title="Locomotive">locomotive</a>, the crew was headed by an <a href="/wiki/Railroad_engineer" title="Railroad engineer">engineer</a>, and included two <a href="/wiki/Fireman_(steam_engine)" title="Fireman (steam engine)">firemen</a> who stoked the boiler with coal, and a craneman. A support crew on the ground of six men extended the rails on which the shovel moved as the digging progressed. A famous photograph of President <a href="/wiki/Theodore_Roosevelt" title="Theodore Roosevelt">Theodore Roosevelt</a> was taken in November 1906 operating a Bucyrus shovel in Panama during his inspection trip to the canal. In March 1910, a single Bucyrus shovel excavated 70,000 cubic yards over twenty-six consecutive days at the <a href="/wiki/Culebra_cut" class="mw-redirect" title="Culebra cut">Culebra cut</a>, setting a canal construction record. Each shovel averaged more than 1,000,000 cubic yards of earth excavated at the cut.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> </p>

      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1927-1980">1927-1980</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bucyrus-Erie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 1927-1980">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The company changed its name to <b>Bucyrus-Erie</b> in 1927 when it merged with the <b>Erie Steam Shovel Company</b>, the country's leading manufacturer of small excavators at that time.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> </p>
      <p>In 1930 Bucyrus joined with the English firm of <a href="/wiki/Ruston_%26_Hornsby" class="mw-redirect" title="Ruston &amp; Hornsby">Ruston &amp; Hornsby</a> Ltd <a href="/wiki/Lincoln,_England" title="Lincoln, England">Lincoln</a>, <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>, to form the <a href="/wiki/Ruston-Bucyrus" title="Ruston-Bucyrus">Ruston-Bucyrus</a> Ltd firm in England. Ruston &amp; Hornsby Ltd were the pre-eminent manufacturers of steam excavators at the time, having started in 1874; the merger gave the company access to previously unavailable world markets. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1980-2011">1980-2011</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bucyrus-Erie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 1980-2011">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>Ruston &amp; Hornsby Ltd sold their share in Ruston-Bucyrus in 1985, during a period of recession and consolidation in the mining industry, as they divested themselves of non-core businesses to survive.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> </p>
      <p>For a time in the 1980s the company was known as Becor Western following its merger with Western Gear.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> </p>
      <p>On February 18, 1994, Bucyrus-Erie filed for <a href="/wiki/Chapter_11,_Title_11,_United_States_Code" title="Chapter 11, Title 11, United States Code">Chapter 11 bankruptcy</a>, and remained under bankruptcy protection until December 14, 1994.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEBucyrus-Erie1994_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEBucyrus-Erie1994-11">[11]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The company took its current name, <b>Bucyrus International, Inc.</b> in 1997.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> </p>
      <p>Bucyrus built hundreds of large mining machines, as well as construction equipment, in an intense competition against competitor <a href="/wiki/Marion_Power_Shovel" class="mw-redirect" title="Marion Power Shovel">Marion Power Shovel</a>. Bucyrus acquired <a href="/wiki/Marion_Power_Shovel" class="mw-redirect" title="Marion Power Shovel">Marion Power Shovel</a> in 1997.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> </p>
      <p>On May 4, 2007, Bucyrus completed the acquisition of the DBT Group, a <a href="/wiki/Lunen,_Germany" class="mw-redirect" title="Lunen, Germany">Lunen, Germany</a> based manufacturer of underground mining equipment, from RAG Coal International AG of <a href="/wiki/Herne,_Germany" class="mw-redirect" title="Herne, Germany">Herne, Germany</a>. Bucyrus acquired DBT because DBT's underground mining equipment complemented Bucyrus' surface mining products.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> </p>
      <p>In February 2010, Bucyrus International completed a US$1.3 billion acquisition of the mining equipment division of <a href="/wiki/Terex" title="Terex">Terex Corporation</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-bucyrus_buys_terex_mining_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bucyrus_buys_terex_mining-12">[12]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>On November 15, 2010, Bucyrus agreed to be acquired by Caterpillar in a transaction valued at US$8.6 billion. Caterpillar said it intended to create a new mining business headquarters at the former Bucyrus headquarters location in South Milwaukee. The transaction closed in mid-2011.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTECaterpillar2010_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTECaterpillar2010-13">[13]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The Intellectual Property Rights for Bucyrus Erie cranes was acquired by Sparrows Group which has crane manufacturing operations based in Houston, Texas <sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Products">Products</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bucyrus-Erie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Products">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Bucyrus owned the Bucyrus, Bucyrus-Erie, Marion, and <a href="/wiki/Ransomes_%26_Rapier" title="Ransomes &amp; Rapier">Ransomes &amp; Rapier</a> brands and provided OEM parts and support services for machinery which bears those brands.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEBucyrus2010a_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEBucyrus2010a-15">[15]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Historical">Historical</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bucyrus-Erie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Historical">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
        <a href="/wiki/File:Bucyrus_Erie_-_geograph.org.uk_-_756712.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/41/Bucyrus_Erie_-_geograph.org.uk_-_756712.jpg/220px-Bucyrus_Erie_-_geograph.org.uk_-_756712.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="146" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/41/Bucyrus_Erie_-_geograph.org.uk_-_756712.jpg/330px-Bucyrus_Erie_-_geograph.org.uk_-_756712.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/41/Bucyrus_Erie_-_geograph.org.uk_-_756712.jpg/440px-Bucyrus_Erie_-_geograph.org.uk_-_756712.jpg 2x" data-file-width="640" data-file-height="424"></a> 
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a href="/wiki/File:Bucyrus_Erie_-_geograph.org.uk_-_756712.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a>
         </div>Bucyrus-Erie 1150RB walking dragline preserved at 
         <a href="/wiki/St_Aidan%27s#Dragline" title="St Aidan's">St Aidan's opencast coal mine</a>, Yorkshire, England
        </div>

      <ul>
       <li>4250-W walking dragline, also known as <a href="/wiki/Big_Muskie" title="Big Muskie">Big Muskie</a>, was built in 1969, with a 220-cubic-yard (170&nbsp;m<sup>3</sup>) bucket and weighed 13,000 metric tons. Big Muskie's 220-cubic-yard (170&nbsp;m<sup>3</sup>) bucket is currently sitting outside McConnellsville, Ohio in a small park dedicated to coal mining.</li> 
       <li>Two 3850-B stripping shovels built in 1962 and 1964, with bucket capacities of 115 and 145&nbsp;cu&nbsp;yd (88 and 111&nbsp;m<sup>3</sup>).</li> 
       <li>The 2570-W or WS, one of B-E's most popular dragline models with bucket capacities between 120 and 160&nbsp;cu&nbsp;yd (92 and 122&nbsp;m<sup>3</sup>). <a href="/wiki/Ursa_Major_(excavator)" title="Ursa Major (excavator)">Ursa Major</a> at <a href="/wiki/Black_Thunder_Coal_Mine" title="Black Thunder Coal Mine">Black Thunder Coal Mine</a> was reported to be the third largest dragline ever built.</li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/The_Silver_Spade" title="The Silver Spade">The Silver Spade</a> and its twin the GEM of Egypt, 1950B Stripping shovels, was built in 1965 and 1967 respectively, with a bucket capacity of 80&nbsp;m<sup>3</sup> (100&nbsp;cu&nbsp;yd). The Silver Spade was dismantled in 2007. Many videos can be seen of it working through Bennetshovel on YouTube.com</li> 
       <li>The Stripping shovel <a href="/wiki/Big_Brutus" title="Big Brutus">Big Brutus</a>, a 1850-B, was built in 1962, with a 90-yard bucket. It currently sits in <a href="/wiki/West_Mineral,_Kansas" title="West Mineral, Kansas">West Mineral</a>, Kansas, as the huge centerpiece of a museum.</li> 
       <li>The Bucyrus-Erie 50-R, a 55-foot-high behemoth rising 11 stories tall and housing a massive drill, was responsible for the successful rescue of two miners during the 1963 Sheppton, Pennsylvania mining disaster.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></li> 
       <li>The 1250-B/W and 1260-W walking draglines, with buckets between 33 and 45&nbsp;cu&nbsp;yd (25 and 34&nbsp;m<sup>3</sup>).</li> 
       <li>The 5-W walking dragline, carrying a 5-cubic-yard (3.8&nbsp;m<sup>3</sup>) bucket and produced until around 1970;</li> 
       <li>Marion Power Shovel Company of <a href="/wiki/Marion,_Ohio" title="Marion, Ohio">Marion</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ohio" title="Ohio">Ohio</a> designed the <a href="/wiki/Crawler_transporter" class="mw-redirect" title="Crawler transporter">crawler transporter</a> used to carry <a href="/wiki/Saturn_V" title="Saturn V">Saturn V</a> rockets and <a href="/wiki/Space_Shuttle" title="Space Shuttle">Space Shuttles</a> to their <a href="/wiki/Launch_pad" title="Launch pad">launch pads</a>.</li> 
       <li>in 1946 an 1150RB walking dragline was transported to Britain as part of the <a href="/wiki/Marshall_Plan" title="Marshall Plan">Marshall Plan</a>. It was used to remove <a href="/wiki/Overburden" title="Overburden">overburden</a> at various mines around the UK, lastly at <a href="/wiki/St_Aidan%27s" title="St Aidan's">St Aidan's</a> opencast coal mine, Yorkshire, until the <a href="/wiki/River_Aire" title="River Aire">River Aire</a> burst its banks and flooded the mine in March 1988. It has been preserved as a static monument at the site.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></li>
      </ul> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Management">Management</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bucyrus-Erie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Management">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
