      <p><b>Duke of Richmond</b> is a title in the <a href="/wiki/Peerage_of_England" title="Peerage of England">Peerage of England</a> that has been created four times in British history. It has been held by members of the royal <a href="/wiki/Tudor_dynasty" class="mw-redirect" title="Tudor dynasty">Tudor</a> and <a href="/wiki/House_of_Stuart" title="House of Stuart">Stuart</a> families. </p>
      <p>The current dukedom of Richmond was created in 1675<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[a]</a></sup> for <a href="/wiki/Charles_Lennox,_1st_Duke_of_Richmond" title="Charles Lennox, 1st Duke of Richmond">Charles Lennox</a>, the illegitimate son of <a href="/wiki/Charles_II_of_England" title="Charles II of England">King Charles II of England</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Breton_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Breton people">Breton</a> noblewoman, <a href="/wiki/Louise_de_K%C3%A9rouaille,_Duchess_of_Portsmouth" title="Louise de Kérouaille, Duchess of Portsmouth">Louise de Penancoët de Kérouaille</a>; Charles Lennox was also made <a href="/wiki/Duke_of_Lennox" title="Duke of Lennox">Duke of Lennox</a> a month later. The <a href="/wiki/Duke_of_Richmond_and_Lennox" title="Duke of Richmond and Lennox">Duke of Richmond and Lennox</a> was furthermore created <a href="/wiki/Duke_of_Gordon" title="Duke of Gordon">Duke of Gordon</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Peerage_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Peerage of the United Kingdom">Peerage of the United Kingdom</a> in 1876, meaning that the Duke holds three dukedoms— plus, in <a href="/wiki/Pretender" title="Pretender">pretence</a>, the French Duchy of <a href="/wiki/Aubigny-sur-N%C3%A8re" title="Aubigny-sur-Nère">Aubigny-sur-Nère</a>— more than any other person in the realm. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History_of_the_Dukedom">History of the Dukedom</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Duke_of_Richmond&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History of the Dukedom">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Prior to the creation of the Dukedom the early nobles of England associated with Richmondshire were <a href="/wiki/Earl_of_Richmond" title="Earl of Richmond">Lords and Earls of Richmond</a>. At times the <a href="/wiki/Honour_of_Richmond" title="Honour of Richmond">honour of Richmond</a> was held without a title. The Dukedom of Richmond emerged under <a href="/wiki/King_Henry_VIII" class="mw-redirect" title="King Henry VIII">King Henry VIII</a>. </p>
      <p>The first creation of a dukedom of Richmond (as <i>Duke of Richmond and Somerset</i>) was made in 1525 for <a href="/wiki/Henry_FitzRoy,_1st_Duke_of_Richmond_and_Somerset" class="mw-redirect" title="Henry FitzRoy, 1st Duke of Richmond and Somerset">Henry FitzRoy</a>, an illegitimate son of <a href="/wiki/Henry_VIII_of_England" title="Henry VIII of England">King Henry VIII</a>. His mother was <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_Blount" title="Elizabeth Blount">Elizabeth Blount</a>. Upon the Duke's death without children in 1536, his titles became extinct. </p>
      <p>The second creation was in 1623 for <a href="/wiki/Ludovic_Stewart,_2nd_Duke_of_Lennox" title="Ludovic Stewart, 2nd Duke of Lennox">Ludovic Stuart</a>, 2nd <a href="/wiki/Duke_of_Lennox" title="Duke of Lennox">Duke of Lennox</a> (see <a href="/wiki/Lennox_(district)" class="mw-redirect" title="Lennox (district)">Lennox (district)</a>) (1574–1624), who also held other titles in the <a href="/wiki/Peerage_of_Scotland" title="Peerage of Scotland">peerage of Scotland</a>. He was created <a href="/wiki/Earl_of_Richmond" title="Earl of Richmond">Earl of Richmond</a> and Baron Settrington in 1613 and Duke of Richmond in the peerage of England in 1623 as a member of the Lennox line (not unlike King James himself) in the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Stuart" title="House of Stuart">House of Stuart</a>. These became extinct at his death in 1624, but his Scottish honours devolved on his brother <a href="/wiki/Esm%C3%A9_Stewart,_3rd_Duke_of_Lennox" title="Esmé Stewart, 3rd Duke of Lennox">Esmé</a>, <a href="/wiki/Earl_of_March" title="Earl of March">Earl of March</a>, who thus became 3rd Duke of Lennox in the peerage of Scotland. Esmé's son <a href="/wiki/James_Stewart,_1st_Duke_of_Richmond" title="James Stewart, 1st Duke of Richmond">James, 4th Duke of Lennox</a> (1612–1655) subsequently received the third creation of the dukedom of Richmond in 1641, when the two dukedoms again became united. In 1672, on the death of James' nephew <a href="/wiki/Charles_Stewart,_3rd_Duke_of_Richmond" title="Charles Stewart, 3rd Duke of Richmond">Charles</a>, 3rd Duke of Richmond and 6th Duke of Lennox, both titles again became extinct.<sup id="cite_ref-EB1911_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EB1911-2">[1]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The fourth creation of the dukedom of Richmond was in August 1675, when <a href="/wiki/Charles_II_of_England" title="Charles II of England">Charles II</a> granted the title to <a href="/wiki/Charles_Lennox,_1st_Duke_of_Richmond" title="Charles Lennox, 1st Duke of Richmond">Charles Lennox</a>, his illegitimate son by <a href="/wiki/Louise_de_Keroualle" class="mw-redirect" title="Louise de Keroualle">Louise de Keroualle</a>, <a href="/wiki/Duchess_of_Portsmouth" class="mw-redirect" title="Duchess of Portsmouth">Duchess of Portsmouth</a>. Charles Lennox was further created Duke of Lennox a month later. Charles' son, also Charles, succeeded to the French title <a href="/wiki/Duke_of_Aubigny" title="Duke of Aubigny">Duke of Aubigny</a> (of <a href="/wiki/Aubigny-sur-N%C3%A8re" title="Aubigny-sur-Nère">Aubigny-sur-Nère</a>) on the death of his grandmother in 1734. The 6th <a href="/wiki/Duke_of_Richmond_and_Lennox" title="Duke of Richmond and Lennox">Duke of Richmond and Lennox</a> was created <a href="/wiki/Duke_of_Gordon" title="Duke of Gordon">Duke of Gordon</a> (See <a href="/wiki/Clan_Gordon" title="Clan Gordon">Clan Gordon</a>) in 1876. Thus, the Duke holds three (four, if the French Aubigny claim is accepted) dukedoms, more than any other person in the realm.<sup id="cite_ref-EB1911_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EB1911-2">[1]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The subsidiary titles of the dukedom created in 1675 are <i>Earl of March</i> (created 1675), <i><a href="/wiki/Earl_of_Darnley" title="Earl of Darnley">Earl of Darnley</a></i> (1675), <i><a href="/wiki/Earl_of_Kinrara" class="mw-redirect" title="Earl of Kinrara">Earl of Kinrara</a></i> (1876), <i>Baron Settrington</i>, of Settrington in the County of York (1675), and <i>Lord Torbolton</i> (1675). </p>

      <p>The Dukes of Richmond, Lennox and Gordon are normally styled <i>Duke of Richmond and Gordon</i>. Before the creation of the Dukedom of Gordon they were styled <i>Duke of Richmond and Lennox</i>. The titles <i>Earl of March</i> and <i>Baron Settrington</i> were created in the peerage of England along with the Dukedom of Richmond. The titles <i><a href="/wiki/Earl_of_Darnley" title="Earl of Darnley">Earl of Darnley</a></i> and <i>Lord Torbolton</i> were created in the <a href="/wiki/Peerage_of_Scotland" title="Peerage of Scotland">Peerage of Scotland</a> along with the Dukedom of Lennox. Finally, the title <i>Earl of Kinrara</i> was created in the peerage of the United Kingdom with the Dukedom of Gordon. The eldest son of the Duke uses the <a href="/wiki/Courtesy_title" title="Courtesy title">courtesy title</a> <i>Earl of March and Kinrara</i>. Before the creation of the Dukedom of Gordon, the courtesy title used was <i>Earl of March</i>. </p>
      <p>The family seat is <a href="/wiki/Goodwood_House" title="Goodwood House">Goodwood House</a> near <a href="/wiki/Chichester" title="Chichester">Chichester</a>, <a href="/wiki/West_Sussex" title="West Sussex">West Sussex</a>. </p>

      <p>The earlier dukes (creations of 1623 and 1641) bore: Quarterly 1 and 4 azure three fleurs-de-lis and a bordure engrailed Or; 2 and 3 Or a fess chequy azure and argent, a bordure gules semy of buckles Or (Stewart of Bonkyl); overall an inescutcheon of Lennox. </p>
      <p>Henry Fitzroy, Duke of Richmond and Somerset (creation of 1525), bore the Tudor royal arms (quarterly France and England) with a border quarterly ermine (for <a href="/wiki/Duke_of_Brittany" class="mw-redirect" title="Duke of Brittany">Brittany</a>) and compony azure and argent (for <a href="/wiki/Duke_of_Somerset" title="Duke of Somerset">Somerset</a>), a baton sinister argent for bastardy, and overall an escutcheon of <a href="/wiki/Earl_of_Nottingham" title="Earl of Nottingham">Nottingham</a>. </p>
