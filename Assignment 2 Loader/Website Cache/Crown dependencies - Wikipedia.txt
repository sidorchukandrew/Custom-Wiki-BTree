      <p>The <b>Crown dependencies</b> (<a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>: <i lang="fr">Dépendances de la Couronne</i>; <a href="/wiki/Manx_language" title="Manx language">Manx</a>: <i lang="gv">Croghaneyn-crooin</i>) are three island territories off the coast of <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">Great Britain</a> that are self-governing possessions of the <a href="/wiki/The_Crown" title="The Crown">Crown</a>: the <a href="/wiki/Bailiwick_of_Guernsey" title="Bailiwick of Guernsey">Bailiwick of Guernsey</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Jersey" title="Jersey">Bailiwick of Jersey</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Isle_of_Man" title="Isle of Man">Isle of Man</a>. They do not form part of either the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a> or the <a href="/wiki/British_Overseas_Territories" title="British Overseas Territories">British Overseas Territories</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Briefing2_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Briefing2-2">[2]</a></sup> Internationally, the dependencies are considered "territories for which the United Kingdom is responsible", rather than <a href="/wiki/Sovereign_state" title="Sovereign state">sovereign states</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-International_recognition_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-International_recognition-3">[3]</a></sup> As a result, they are not member states of the <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_of_Nations" title="Commonwealth of Nations">Commonwealth of Nations</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-justice.gov.uk_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-justice.gov.uk-4">[4]</a></sup> However, they do have relationships with the Commonwealth, the <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a>, and other international organisations, and are members of the <a href="/wiki/British%E2%80%93Irish_Council" title="British–Irish Council">British–Irish Council</a>. They have their own teams in the <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_Games" title="Commonwealth Games">Commonwealth Games</a>. They are not part of the European Union (EU), although they are within the <a href="/wiki/European_Union_Customs_Union" title="European Union Customs Union">EU's customs area</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Isle_of_Man" title="Isle of Man">Isle of Man</a> (along with the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>) is within the <a href="/wiki/European_Union_value_added_tax_area" class="mw-redirect" title="European Union value added tax area">EU's VAT area</a>. </p>
      <p>As the Crown dependencies are not sovereign states, the power to pass legislation affecting the islands ultimately rests with the government of the United Kingdom (though this is rarely done without the consent of the dependencies, and the right to do so is disputed). However they each have their own legislative assembly, with the power to legislate on many local matters with the assent of the Crown (<a href="/wiki/Her_Majesty%27s_Most_Honourable_Privy_Council" class="mw-redirect" title="Her Majesty's Most Honourable Privy Council">Privy Council</a>, or in the case of the Isle of Man in certain circumstances the <a href="/wiki/Lieutenant_Governor_of_the_Isle_of_Man" title="Lieutenant Governor of the Isle of Man">Lieutenant-Governor</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> In each case, the <a href="/wiki/Head_of_government" title="Head of government">head of government</a> is referred to as the <a href="/wiki/Chief_Minister" class="mw-redirect" title="Chief Minister">Chief Minister</a>. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Definition">Definition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Crown_dependencies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Definition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>"The Crown" is defined differently in each Crown Dependency. In Jersey, statements in the 21st century of the constitutional position by the <a href="/wiki/Law_Officers_of_the_Crown" class="mw-redirect" title="Law Officers of the Crown">Law Officers of the Crown</a> define it as the "Crown in right of Jersey",<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> with all Crown land in the Bailiwick of Jersey belonging to the Crown in right of Jersey and not to the <a href="/wiki/Crown_Estate" title="Crown Estate">Crown Estate</a> of the United Kingdom.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Legislation of the Isle of Man defines the "Crown in right of the Isle of Man" as being separate from the "Crown in right of the United Kingdom".<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> In Guernsey, legislation refers to the "Crown in right of the Bailiwick",<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> and the Law Officers of the Crown of Guernsey submitted that "The Crown in this context ordinarily means the Crown in right of the <i>république</i> of the Bailiwick of Guernsey"<sup id="cite_ref-RJCO2010_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RJCO2010-10">[10]</a></sup> and that this comprises "the collective governmental and civic institutions, established by and under the authority of the Monarch, for the governance of these Islands, including the States of Guernsey and legislatures in the other Islands, the Royal Court and other courts, the Lieutenant Governor, Parish authorities, and the Crown acting in and through the Privy Council."<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> This constitutional concept is also worded as the "Crown in right of the Bailiwick of Guernsey".<sup id="cite_ref-RJCO2010_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RJCO2010-10">[10]</a></sup> </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Channel_Islands:_Bailiwicks_of_Jersey_and_Guernsey">Channel Islands: Bailiwicks of Jersey and Guernsey</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Crown_dependencies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Channel Islands: Bailiwicks of Jersey and Guernsey">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       Main article: 
       <a href="/wiki/Channel_Islands" title="Channel Islands">Channel Islands</a>
      </div> 
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
        <a href="/wiki/File:Wyspy_Normandzkie.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c1/Wyspy_Normandzkie.png/220px-Wyspy_Normandzkie.png" decoding="async" width="220" height="307" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c1/Wyspy_Normandzkie.png/330px-Wyspy_Normandzkie.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c1/Wyspy_Normandzkie.png/440px-Wyspy_Normandzkie.png 2x" data-file-width="1419" data-file-height="1980"></a> 
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a href="/wiki/File:Wyspy_Normandzkie.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a>
         </div>The 
         <a href="/wiki/Channel_Islands" title="Channel Islands">Channel Islands</a> located in the 
         <a href="/wiki/English_Channel" title="English Channel">English Channel</a>
        </div>
       </div>
      </div> 
      <p>Since 1290,<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> the Channel Islands have been governed as </p> 
      <ul>
       <li>the <a href="/wiki/Bailiwick_of_Jersey" class="mw-redirect" title="Bailiwick of Jersey">Bailiwick of Jersey</a>, comprising the island of <a href="/wiki/Jersey" title="Jersey">Jersey</a> and uninhabited islets such as the <a href="/wiki/Minquiers" title="Minquiers">Minquiers</a> and <a href="/wiki/%C3%89cr%C3%A9hous" title="Écréhous">Écréhous</a></li> 
       <li>the <a href="/wiki/Bailiwick_of_Guernsey" title="Bailiwick of Guernsey">Bailiwick of Guernsey</a>, comprising the islands of <a href="/wiki/Guernsey" title="Guernsey">Guernsey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sark" title="Sark">Sark</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alderney" title="Alderney">Alderney</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brecqhou" title="Brecqhou">Brecqhou</a>, <a href="/wiki/Herm" title="Herm">Herm</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jethou" title="Jethou">Jethou</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lihou" title="Lihou">Lihou</a>.</li>
      </ul> 
      <p>Each Bailiwick is a Crown dependency and each is headed by a Bailiff, with a Lieutenant Governor representing the Crown in each Bailiwick. Each Bailiwick has its own legal and healthcare systems, and its own separate immigration policies, with "local status" in one Bailiwick having no jurisdiction in the other. The two Bailiwicks exercise bilateral <a href="/wiki/Tax_treaty" title="Tax treaty">double taxation treaties</a>. Since 1961, the Bailiwicks have had separate courts of <a href="/wiki/Appeal" title="Appeal">appeal</a>, but generally the Bailiff of each Bailiwick has been appointed to serve on the panel of appellate judges for the other Bailiwick. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bailiwick_of_Guernsey">Bailiwick of Guernsey</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Crown_dependencies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Bailiwick of Guernsey">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       Main article: 
       <a href="/wiki/Bailiwick_of_Guernsey" title="Bailiwick of Guernsey">Bailiwick of Guernsey</a>
      </div> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       See also: 
       <a href="/wiki/List_of_islands_of_the_Bailiwick_of_Guernsey" title="List of islands of the Bailiwick of Guernsey">List of islands of the Bailiwick of Guernsey</a>
      </div> 
      <p>The Bailiwick of Guernsey comprises three separate jurisdictions: </p> 
      <ul>
       <li><a href="/wiki/Guernsey" title="Guernsey">Guernsey</a>, which includes also the nearby islands of Herm and Jethou, and other smaller uninhabited islands.</li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/Sark" title="Sark">Sark</a>, which also includes the nearby island of Brecqhou, and other smaller uninhabited islands.</li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/Alderney" title="Alderney">Alderney</a>, including smaller surrounding uninhabited islands.</li>
      </ul> 
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
        <a href="/wiki/File:Brecqhou_from_the_air.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/16/Brecqhou_from_the_air.JPG/220px-Brecqhou_from_the_air.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="140" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/16/Brecqhou_from_the_air.JPG/330px-Brecqhou_from_the_air.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/16/Brecqhou_from_the_air.JPG/440px-Brecqhou_from_the_air.JPG 2x" data-file-width="3978" data-file-height="2535"></a> 
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a href="/wiki/File:Brecqhou_from_the_air.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a>
         </div>
         <a href="/wiki/Brecqhou" title="Brecqhou">Brecqhou</a> island
        </div>
       </div>
      </div> 
      <p>The parliament of Guernsey is the <a href="/wiki/States_of_Guernsey#States_of_Deliberation" title="States of Guernsey">States of Deliberation</a>, the parliament of Sark is called the <a href="/wiki/Sark#Chief_Pleas" title="Sark">Chief Pleas</a>, and the parliament of Alderney is called the <a href="/wiki/States_of_Alderney" title="States of Alderney">States of Alderney</a>. The three parliaments together can also approve joint Bailiwick-wide legislation that applies in those parts of the Bailiwick whose parliaments approve it. </p>
      <p>Guernsey issues its own <a href="/wiki/Coin" title="Coin">coins</a> and <a href="/wiki/Banknote" title="Banknote">banknotes</a>: </p> 
      <ul>
       <li><a href="/wiki/Guernsey_pound" title="Guernsey pound">Guernsey banknotes</a></li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/Coins_of_the_Guernsey_pound" class="mw-redirect" title="Coins of the Guernsey pound">Coins of the Guernsey pound</a></li>
      </ul> 
      <p>These circulate freely in both Bailiwicks alongside <a href="/wiki/Pound_sterling" title="Pound sterling">UK coinage</a> and <a href="/wiki/Banknotes_of_the_pound_sterling" title="Banknotes of the pound sterling">English and Scottish banknotes</a>. They are not <a href="/wiki/Pound_sterling#Legal_tender_and_regional_issues" title="Pound sterling">legal tender</a> within the UK.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>There are no <a href="/wiki/Political_parties" class="mw-redirect" title="Political parties">political parties</a> in any of the parliaments; candidates stand for election as <a href="/wiki/Independent_(politics)" class="mw-redirect" title="Independent (politics)">independents</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Guernsey has its own separate <a href="/wiki/List_of_international_license_plate_codes" class="mw-redirect" title="List of international license plate codes">international vehicle registrations</a> (GBG – Guernsey, GBA – Alderney), <a href="/wiki/List_of_Internet_TLDs" class="mw-redirect" title="List of Internet TLDs">internet domain</a> (<a href="/wiki/.gg" title=".gg">.gg</a> – Guernsey), and <a href="/wiki/ISO_3166-2" title="ISO 3166-2">ISO 3166-2</a> codes, first reserved on behalf of the <a href="/wiki/Universal_Postal_Union" title="Universal Postal Union">Universal Postal Union</a> (GGY – Guernsey) and then added officially by the <a href="/wiki/International_Organization_for_Standardization" title="International Organization for Standardization">International Organization for Standardization</a> on 29 March 2006. In any case the GBG on a numberplate is only put on the number plate of a car or motorbike at the request of the vehicle owner and is not compulsory, however a motorbike/scooter can have an identical number to a car, e.g. 5432 on 2 wheels and on 4 wheels. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bailiwick_of_Jersey">Bailiwick of Jersey</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Crown_dependencies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Bailiwick of Jersey">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">

      <p>The <a href="/wiki/Bailiwick_of_Jersey" class="mw-redirect" title="Bailiwick of Jersey">Bailiwick of Jersey</a> consists of the island of Jersey and a number of surrounding uninhabited islands. </p>
      <p>The parliament is the <a href="/wiki/States_of_Jersey" class="mw-redirect" title="States of Jersey">States of Jersey</a>, the first known mention of which is in a document of 1497.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> The <i>States of Jersey Law 2005</i> introduced the post of <a href="/wiki/Chief_Minister_of_Jersey" title="Chief Minister of Jersey">Chief Minister of Jersey</a>, abolished the Bailiff's power of dissent to a resolution of the States and the Lieutenant Governor's power of veto over a resolution of the States, established that any Order in Council or Act of the United Kingdom proposed to apply to Jersey must be referred to the States so that the States can express their views on it.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Jersey issues its own coins and banknotes: </p> 
      <ul>
       <li><a href="/wiki/Jersey_pound" title="Jersey pound">Jersey banknotes</a></li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/Coins_of_the_Jersey_pound" class="mw-redirect" title="Coins of the Jersey pound">Coins of the Jersey pound</a></li>
      </ul> 
      <p>These circulate freely in both Bailiwicks alongside UK coinage and English and Scottish banknotes. They are not <a href="/wiki/Legal_tender" title="Legal tender">legal tender</a> within the UK but are legal currency backed by deposits at the Bank of England. </p>
      <p>There are few <a href="/wiki/Political_parties" class="mw-redirect" title="Political parties">political parties</a>, as candidates generally stand for election as independents (but see <i><a href="/wiki/List_of_political_parties_in_Jersey" title="List of political parties in Jersey">List of political parties in Jersey</a></i>). </p>
      <p>Jersey has its own separate <a href="/wiki/List_of_international_license_plate_codes" class="mw-redirect" title="List of international license plate codes">international vehicle registration</a> (GBJ – Jersey), <a href="/wiki/List_of_Internet_TLDs" class="mw-redirect" title="List of Internet TLDs">internet domain</a> (<a href="/wiki/.je" title=".je">.je</a> – Jersey), and <a href="/wiki/ISO_3166-2" title="ISO 3166-2">ISO 3166-2</a> codes, first reserved on behalf of the <a href="/wiki/Universal_Postal_Union" title="Universal Postal Union">Universal Postal Union</a> (JEY – Jersey) and then added officially by the <a href="/wiki/International_Organization_for_Standardization" title="International Organization for Standardization">International Organization for Standardization</a> on 29 March 2006. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Isle_of_Man">Isle of Man</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Crown_dependencies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Isle of Man">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;">
        <a href="/wiki/File:The_Isle_of_Man.svg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/35/The_Isle_of_Man.svg/200px-The_Isle_of_Man.svg.png" decoding="async" width="200" height="300" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/35/The_Isle_of_Man.svg/300px-The_Isle_of_Man.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/35/The_Isle_of_Man.svg/400px-The_Isle_of_Man.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="650" data-file-height="976"></a>

         <div class="magnify">
          <a href="/wiki/File:The_Isle_of_Man.svg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a>
         </div>The 
         <a href="/wiki/Isle_of_Man" title="Isle of Man">Isle of Man</a> located in the 
         <a href="/wiki/Irish_Sea" title="Irish Sea">Irish Sea</a>

      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">

       <a href="/wiki/Isle_of_Man" title="Isle of Man">Isle of Man</a>

      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">

       <a href="/wiki/Politics_of_the_Isle_of_Man" title="Politics of the Isle of Man">Politics of the Isle of Man</a>,

      <p>The Isle of Man's <a href="/wiki/Tynwald" title="Tynwald">Tynwald</a> claims to be the world's oldest <a href="/wiki/Parliament" title="Parliament">parliament</a> in continuous existence, dating back to 979. (However, it does not claim to be the oldest parliament, as <a href="/wiki/Iceland" title="Iceland">Iceland</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Althing" title="Althing">Althing</a> dates back to 930.) It consists of a popularly elected <i><a href="/wiki/House_of_Keys" title="House of Keys">House of Keys</a></i> and an indirectly elected <i><a href="/wiki/Legislative_Council_of_the_Isle_of_Man" title="Legislative Council of the Isle of Man">Legislative Council</a></i>, which may sit separately or jointly to consider pieces of legislation, which, when passed into law, are known as "<a href="/wiki/Act_of_Tynwald" title="Act of Tynwald">Acts of Tynwald</a>". Candidates mostly stand for election to the Keys as independents, rather than being selected by <a href="/wiki/Political_parties" class="mw-redirect" title="Political parties">political parties</a>. There is a Council of Ministers headed by a <a href="/wiki/Chief_Minister_of_the_Isle_of_Man" title="Chief Minister of the Isle of Man">Chief Minister</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The Isle of Man issues its own coins and banknotes: </p> 
      <ul>
       <li><a href="/wiki/Manx_pound" title="Manx pound">Manx banknotes</a></li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/Coins_of_the_Manx_pound" title="Coins of the Manx pound">Coins of the Manx pound</a></li>
      </ul> 
      <p>These circulate freely on the island alongside British coinage (as well as coinage from the Channel Islands and Gibraltar) and English, Northern Irish and Scottish banknotes. </p>
      <p>The Isle of Man, unlike the other Crown dependencies, has a <a href="/wiki/Common_Purse_Agreement" title="Common Purse Agreement">Common Purse Agreement</a> with the United Kingdom. </p>
      <p><a href="/wiki/Isle_of_Man_Post" class="mw-redirect" title="Isle of Man Post">Isle of Man Post</a> issues its own stamps and derives significant revenue from the sale of special issues to collectors. </p>
      <p>The Isle of Man has its own separate <a href="/wiki/List_of_international_license_plate_codes" class="mw-redirect" title="List of international license plate codes">international vehicle registration</a> (GBM – Isle of Man), <a href="/wiki/List_of_Internet_TLDs" class="mw-redirect" title="List of Internet TLDs">internet domain</a> (<a href="/wiki/.im" title=".im">.im</a> – Isle of Man), and <a href="/wiki/ISO_3166-2" title="ISO 3166-2">ISO 3166-2</a> codes, first reserved on behalf of the <a href="/wiki/Universal_Postal_Union" title="Universal Postal Union">Universal Postal Union</a> (IMN – Isle of Man) and then added officially by the <a href="/wiki/International_Organization_for_Standardization" title="International Organization for Standardization">International Organization for Standardization</a> on 29 March 2006. In addition, since 2008 the Isle of Man has used the <a href="/wiki/Aircraft_registration" title="Aircraft registration">aircraft registration</a> M-. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Relationship_with_the_British_Crown">Relationship with the British Crown</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Crown_dependencies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Relationship with the British Crown">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>In each Crown dependency, the monarch is represented by a <a href="/wiki/Lieutenant_Governor" class="mw-redirect" title="Lieutenant Governor">Lieutenant Governor</a>, but this post is largely ceremonial. Since 2010 the Lieutenant Governors of each Crown dependency have been recommended to the Crown by a panel in each respective Crown dependency; this replaced the previous system of the appointments being made by the Crown on the recommendation of UK ministers.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> In 2005, it was decided in the Isle of Man to replace the Lieutenant Governor with a <a href="/wiki/Crown_Commissioner" title="Crown Commissioner">Crown Commissioner</a>, but this decision was reversed before it was implemented. </p>
      <p>All "insular" legislation has to receive the approval of the "Queen in Council", in effect, the <a href="/wiki/Privy_Council_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Privy Council of the United Kingdom">Privy Council</a> in London.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> Certain types of domestic legislation in the Isle of Man, however, may be signed into law by the Lieutenant Governor, using delegated powers, without having to pass through the Privy Council. In Jersey, provisional legislation of an administrative nature may be adopted by means of <i>triennial regulations</i> (renewable after three years), without requiring the assent of the Privy Council.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> Much legislation, in practice, is effected by means of <a href="/wiki/Secondary_legislation" class="mw-redirect" title="Secondary legislation">secondary legislation</a> under the authority of prior laws or Orders in Council. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bailiwicks_of_Guernsey_and_Jersey">Bailiwicks of Guernsey and Jersey</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Crown_dependencies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Bailiwicks of Guernsey and Jersey">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;">
        <a href="/wiki/File:La_Reine_Notre_Duc_2012.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/78/La_Reine_Notre_Duc_2012.jpg/170px-La_Reine_Notre_Duc_2012.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="317" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/78/La_Reine_Notre_Duc_2012.jpg/255px-La_Reine_Notre_Duc_2012.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/78/La_Reine_Notre_Duc_2012.jpg/340px-La_Reine_Notre_Duc_2012.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1872" data-file-height="3487"></a> 
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a href="/wiki/File:La_Reine_Notre_Duc_2012.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a>
         </div>"La Reine, Notre Duc" (The Queen, Our Duke): title of a Diamond Jubilee exhibition at the Jersey Arts Centre in 2012
        </div>
       </div>
      </div> 
      <p>The Channel Islands are part of the territory annexed by the <a href="/wiki/Duchy_of_Normandy" title="Duchy of Normandy">Duchy of Normandy</a> in 933 from the <a href="/wiki/Duchy_of_Brittany" title="Duchy of Brittany">Duchy of Brittany</a>. This territory was added to the grant of land given in settlement by the King of France in 911 to the Viking raiders who had sailed up the Seine almost to the walls of Paris. </p>
      <p><a href="/wiki/William_the_Conqueror" title="William the Conqueror">William the Conqueror</a>, Duke of Normandy, claimed the title <a href="/wiki/King_of_England" class="mw-redirect" title="King of England">King of England</a> in 1066, following the death of <a href="/wiki/Edward_the_Confessor" title="Edward the Confessor">Edward the Confessor</a>, and secured the claim through the <a href="/wiki/Norman_conquest_of_England" title="Norman conquest of England">Norman conquest of England</a>. </p>
      <p>Subsequent marriages between Kings of England and French nobles meant that Kings of England had title to more French lands than the King of France. When the King of France asserted his feudal right of <a href="/wiki/Jus_patronatus" title="Jus patronatus">patronage</a>, the then-King of England, King <a href="/wiki/John_of_England" class="mw-redirect" title="John of England">John</a>, fearing he would be imprisoned should he attend, failed to fulfil his obligation. </p>
      <p>In 1204 the title and lands of the Duchy of <a href="/wiki/Normandy" title="Normandy">Normandy</a> and his other French possessions were stripped from King <a href="/wiki/John_of_England" class="mw-redirect" title="John of England">John of England</a> by the King of France. The Channel Islands remained in the possession of the King of England, who ruled them as Duke of Normandy until the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Paris_(1259)" title="Treaty of Paris (1259)">Treaty of Paris</a> in 1259. </p>
      <p>John's son, <a href="/wiki/Henry_III_of_England" title="Henry III of England">Henry III</a>, renounced the title of Duke of Normandy by that treaty, and none of his successors ever revived it. The Channel Islands continued to be governed by the Kings of England as French fiefs, distinct from Normandy, until the <a href="/wiki/Hundred_Years%27_War" title="Hundred Years' War">Hundred Years' War</a>, during which they were definitively separated from France. </p>
      <p>At no time did the Channel Islands form part of the Kingdom of England, and they remained legally separate, though under the same monarch, through the subsequent unions of Scotland and England (1707), and Ireland (1801). <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_II_of_the_United_Kingdom" class="mw-redirect" title="Elizabeth II of the United Kingdom">Elizabeth II</a> reigns over the Channel Islands directly, and not by virtue of her role as monarch of the United Kingdom. No specific title is associated with her role as monarch of the Channel Islands, however; she is popularly referred to (even on a Buckingham Palace website) as "Duke of Normandy"<sup id="cite_ref-page2601_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-page2601-22">[22]</a></sup> (not "Duchess")<sup id="cite_ref-page5744_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-page5744-23">[23]</a></sup> but this anachronistic title has no basis in law.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> The monarch has been described, in Jersey, as the "Queen in right of Jersey",<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> and in legislation as the "Sovereign of the Bailiwick of Jersey" and "Sovereign in right of the Bailiwick of Jersey".<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>A unique constitutional position has arisen as successive monarchs have confirmed the <a href="/wiki/Royal_charters_applying_to_the_Channel_Islands" title="Royal charters applying to the Channel Islands">liberties and privileges of the Bailiwicks</a>, often referring to the so-called <i>Constitutions of King John</i>, a legendary document supposed to have been granted by King John in the aftermath of 1204. Governments of the Bailiwicks have generally tried to avoid testing the limits of the unwritten constitution by avoiding conflict with British governments. </p>
      <p>Following the restoration of <a href="/wiki/Charles_II_of_England" title="Charles II of England">King Charles II</a>, who had spent part of his exile in Jersey, the Channel Islands were given the right to set their own customs duties, referred to by the <a href="/wiki/Jersey_Legal_French" title="Jersey Legal French">Jersey Legal French</a> term as <i>impôts</i>. </p>

      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Isle_of_Man_2">Isle of Man</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Crown_dependencies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Isle of Man">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>In the <a href="/wiki/Isle_of_Man" title="Isle of Man">Isle of Man</a> the British monarch is <a href="/wiki/Lord_of_Mann" title="Lord of Mann">Lord of Mann</a>, a title variously held by <a href="/wiki/Norsemen" title="Norsemen">Norse</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scottish</a> and English kings and nobles (the English nobles in <a href="/wiki/Feudality" class="mw-redirect" title="Feudality">feudality</a> to the English Crown) until it was revested into the British monarchy in 1765. The title "Lord" is today used irrespective of the gender of the person who holds it. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Relationship_with_the_UK">Relationship with the UK</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Crown_dependencies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Relationship with the UK">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Crown dependencies have the international status of "territories for which the United Kingdom is responsible" rather than sovereign states.<sup id="cite_ref-International_recognition_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-International_recognition-3">[3]</a></sup> The relationship between the Crown dependencies and the UK is "one of mutual respect and support, i.e. a partnership".<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Until 2001, responsibility for the UK government's relationships with the Crown dependencies rested with the <a href="/wiki/Home_Office" title="Home Office">Home Office</a>, but it was then transferred first to the <a href="/wiki/Lord_Chancellor%27s_Department" title="Lord Chancellor's Department">Lord Chancellor's Department</a>, then to the <a href="/wiki/Department_for_Constitutional_Affairs" title="Department for Constitutional Affairs">Department for Constitutional Affairs</a>, and finally to the <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Justice_(United_Kingdom)" title="Ministry of Justice (United Kingdom)">Ministry of Justice</a>. In 2010 the Ministry of Justice stated that relationships with the Crown dependencies are the responsibility of the United Kingdom Government as a whole, with the Ministry of Justice holding responsibility for the constitutional relationship and other ministries engaging with their opposite numbers in the Crown dependencies according to their respective policy areas.<sup id="cite_ref-justice.gov.uk_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-justice.gov.uk-4">[4]</a></sup> </p>

      <p>The British Government is solely responsible for defence and international representation<sup id="cite_ref-Briefing2_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Briefing2-2">[2]</a></sup> (although, in accordance with 2007 framework agreements,<sup id="cite_ref-FramewJersey_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FramewJersey-29">[29]</a></sup> the UK has elected not to act internationally on behalf of the Crown dependencies without prior consultation). The Crown dependencies are within the <a href="/wiki/Common_Travel_Area" title="Common Travel Area">Common Travel Area</a> and apply the same <a href="/wiki/Visa_policy_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Visa policy of the United Kingdom">visa policy</a> as the United Kingdom, but each Crown dependency has responsibility for its own customs and immigration services. </p>
      <p>Acts of the <a href="/wiki/British_Parliament" class="mw-redirect" title="British Parliament">British Parliament</a> do not usually apply to the Channel Islands and the Isle of Man, unless explicitly stated. UK legislation does not ordinarily extend to them without their consent.<sup id="cite_ref-justice.gov.uk_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-justice.gov.uk-4">[4]</a></sup> For a UK Act to extend otherwise than by an <a href="/wiki/Order_in_Council" title="Order in Council">Order in Council</a> is now very unusual.<sup id="cite_ref-Briefing2_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Briefing2-2">[2]</a></sup> When deemed advisable, Acts of Parliament may be extended to the Islands by means of an Order in Council (thus giving the UK Government some responsibility for <a href="/wiki/Good_governance" title="Good governance">good governance</a> in the islands). An example of this was the <a href="/wiki/Television_Act_1954" title="Television Act 1954">Television Act 1954</a>, which was extended to the Channel Islands, so as to create a local <a href="/wiki/ITV_(TV_network)" title="ITV (TV network)">ITV</a> franchise, known as <a href="/wiki/Channel_Television" class="mw-redirect" title="Channel Television">Channel Television</a>. By constitutional convention this is only done at the request of the Insular Authorities,<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> and has become a rare option (thus giving the Insular Authorities themselves the responsibility for good governance in the islands), the islands usually preferring nowadays to pass localised versions of laws giving effect to international treaties. </p>
      <p>Westminster retains the right to legislate for the Islands against their will as a last resort, but this is also rarely exercised, and may, according to legal opinion from the Attorney-General of Jersey, have fallen into <a href="/wiki/Desuetude" title="Desuetude">desuetude</a> — although this argument was not accepted by the Department for Constitutional Affairs. (The <a href="/wiki/Marine_Broadcasting_Offences_Act" class="mw-redirect" title="Marine Broadcasting Offences Act">Marine, Etc., Broadcasting (Offences) Act 1967</a> was one recent piece of legislation extended to the Isle of Man against the wishes of Tynwald). </p>
      <p>The States of Jersey Law 2005 established that all Acts of the United Kingdom and Orders in Council were to be referred to the <a href="/wiki/States_of_Jersey" class="mw-redirect" title="States of Jersey">States</a>, thus giving greater freedom of action to Jersey in international affairs. </p>
      <p>As in England, but not the United Kingdom as a whole, the <a href="/wiki/Church_of_England" title="Church of England">Church of England</a> is the <a href="/wiki/State_religion" title="State religion">Established Church</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Isle_of_Man" title="Isle of Man">Isle of Man</a>, <a href="/wiki/Guernsey" title="Guernsey">Guernsey</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jersey" title="Jersey">Jersey</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>In 2007–2008, each Crown Dependency and the UK signed agreements<sup id="cite_ref-FramewJersey_29-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FramewJersey-29">[29]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> that established frameworks for the development of the international identity of each Crown Dependency. Among the points clarified in the agreements were that: </p> 
      <ul>
       <li>the UK has no democratic accountability in and for the Crown Dependencies, which are governed by their own democratically elected assemblies;</li> 
       <li>the UK will not act internationally on behalf of the Crown Dependencies without prior consultation;</li> 
       <li>each Crown Dependency has an international identity that is different from that of the UK;</li> 
       <li>the UK supports the principle of each Crown Dependency further developing its international identity;</li> 
       <li>the UK recognises that the interests of each Crown Dependency may differ from those of the UK, and the UK will seek to represent any differing interests when acting in an international capacity; and</li> 
       <li>the UK and each Crown Dependency will work together to resolve or clarify any differences that may arise between their respective interests.</li>
      </ul> 
      <p>The constitutional and cultural proximity of the Islands to the UK means that there are shared institutions and organisations. The <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a>, for example, has local radio stations in the Channel Islands, and also a website run by a team based in the Isle of Man (which is included in <a href="/wiki/BBC_North_West" title="BBC North West">BBC North West</a>). While the Islands now assume responsibility for their own post and telecommunications, they continue to participate in the <a href="/wiki/Telephone_numbers_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="Telephone numbers in the United Kingdom">UK telephone numbering plan</a>, and they have adopted <a href="/wiki/Postal_codes_in_the_United_Kingdom" class="mw-redirect" title="Postal codes in the United Kingdom">postcode</a> systems that are compatible with that of the UK. </p>
      <p>The Crown dependencies, together with the United Kingdom, are collectively <a href="/wiki/Terminology_of_the_British_Isles" title="Terminology of the British Isles">known as</a> the <a href="/wiki/British_Islands" title="British Islands">British Islands</a>. Since the <a href="/wiki/British_Nationality_Act_1981" title="British Nationality Act 1981">British Nationality Act 1981</a> came into effect, they have been treated as part of the United Kingdom for <a href="/wiki/British_nationality_law" title="British nationality law">British nationality law</a> purposes.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> However, each Crown dependency maintains local controls over housing and employment, with special rules applying to British citizens without specified connections to that Crown dependency (as well as to non-British citizens). </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Relationship_with_the_Commonwealth_of_Nations">Relationship with the Commonwealth of Nations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Crown_dependencies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Relationship with the Commonwealth of Nations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>While their constitutional status bears some resemblance to that of the <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_realms" class="mw-redirect" title="Commonwealth realms">Commonwealth realms</a>, the Crown dependencies are not members of the <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_of_Nations" title="Commonwealth of Nations">Commonwealth of Nations</a>. They participate in the Commonwealth of Nations by virtue of their relationship with the United Kingdom, and participate in various Commonwealth institutions in their own right. For example, all three participate in the <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_Parliamentary_Association" title="Commonwealth Parliamentary Association">Commonwealth Parliamentary Association</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_Games" title="Commonwealth Games">Commonwealth Games</a>. </p>
      <p>All three Crown dependencies regard the existing situation as unsatisfactory and have lobbied for change. The States of Jersey have called on the British Foreign Secretary to request that the <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_Heads_of_Government" class="mw-redirect" title="Commonwealth Heads of Government">Commonwealth Heads of Government</a> "consider granting associate membership to Jersey and the other Crown Dependencies as well as any other territories at a similarly advanced stage of autonomy". Jersey has proposed that it be accorded "self-representation in all Commonwealth meetings; full participation in debates and procedures, with a right to speak where relevant and the opportunity to enter into discussions with those who are full members; and no right to vote in the Ministerial or Heads of Government meetings, which is reserved for full members".<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> The States of Guernsey and the Government of the Isle of Man have made calls of a similar nature for a more integrated relationship with the Commonwealth,<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> including more direct representation and enhanced participation in Commonwealth organisations and meetings, including <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_Heads_of_Government_Meeting" title="Commonwealth Heads of Government Meeting">Commonwealth Heads of Government Meetings</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Chief_Minister_of_the_Isle_of_Man" title="Chief Minister of the Isle of Man">Chief Minister of the Isle of Man</a> has said: "A closer connection with the Commonwealth itself would be a welcome further development of the Island's international relationships"<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Relationship_with_the_EU">Relationship with the EU</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Crown_dependencies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Relationship with the EU">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Certain aspects of membership of the <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a> apply to the Crown dependencies, by association of the United Kingdom's membership. For example, Article 355 (5)(c) of the Treaty on the Functioning of the European Union (TFEU) states: </p> 
      <dl>
       <dd>
        <i>this Treaty shall apply to the Channel Islands and the Isle of Man only to the extent necessary to ensure the implementation of the arrangements for those islands set out in the Treaty concerning the accession of new Member States to the European Economic Community and to the European Atomic Energy Community signed on 22 January 1972.</i>;
       </dd>
      </dl> 
      <p>and by <a href="/wiki/Protocol_(treaty)" class="mw-redirect" title="Protocol (treaty)">Protocol</a> 3 to the UK's Act of Accession to the Community:<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> </p> 
      <blockquote>
       <p>An Act to make provision in connection with the enlargement of the European Communities to include the United Kingdom, together with (for certain purposes) the Channel Islands, the Isle of Man and <a href="/wiki/Gibraltar" title="Gibraltar">Gibraltar</a>. [17 October 1972]"</p>
      </blockquote> 
      <p>Of the <a href="/wiki/Four_Freedoms_(European_Union)" class="mw-redirect" title="Four Freedoms (European Union)">Four Freedoms of the EU</a>, the islands take part in that concerning the movement of goods, but not those concerning the movement of persons, services or capital. The <a href="/wiki/Channel_Islands" title="Channel Islands">Channel Islands</a> are outside the <a href="/wiki/Value_Added_Tax" class="mw-redirect" title="Value Added Tax">VAT</a> area (as they have no VAT), while the Isle of Man is inside it.<sup id="cite_ref-vatcustoms_41-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vatcustoms-41">[41]</a></sup> Both areas are inside the customs union.<sup id="cite_ref-customs_42-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-customs-42">[42]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Channel Islanders and Manx people are British citizens and hence <a href="/wiki/European_citizen" class="mw-redirect" title="European citizen">European citizens</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> However, they are not entitled to take advantage of the freedom of movement of people or services unless they are directly connected (through birth, descent from a parent or grandparent, or five years' residence) with the United Kingdom.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>The <a href="/wiki/Common_agricultural_policy" class="mw-redirect" title="Common agricultural policy">common agricultural policy</a> of the EU does not apply to the Crown dependencies. Their citizens do not take part in elections to the <a href="/wiki/European_Parliament" title="European Parliament">European Parliament</a>. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Brexit">Brexit</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Crown_dependencies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Brexit">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>With the <a href="/wiki/Brexit" title="Brexit">Brexit</a> negotiations, the House of Lords has produced a report titled "Brexit: the Crown Dependencies", which states that the "UK Government must continue to fulfil its constitutional obligations to represent the interests of the Crown Dependencies in international relations, even where these differ from those of the UK, both during the Brexit negotiations and beyond." <sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup> In the <a href="/wiki/Great_Repeal_Bill_2016" class="mw-redirect" title="Great Repeal Bill 2016">Great Repeal Bill</a> <a href="/wiki/White_paper" title="White paper">white paper</a> published on 30 March 2017 the UK government states "The Government is committed to engaging with the Crown Dependencies, Gibraltar and the other Overseas Territories as we leave the EU."<sup id="cite_ref-GRBWP_46-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GRBWP-46">[46]</a></sup><sup class="reference" style="white-space:nowrap;">:<span>ch.5</span></sup> </p>
