      <p>In <a href="/wiki/Somali_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Somali people">Somali</a> society, each individual has a <a href="/wiki/Given_name" title="Given name">given name</a>. Sometimes it is presented along with the father's given name, and sometimes the paternal grandfather's given name is attached as well. Somalis do not have surnames or family names.<sup id="cite_ref-9780873386586-pp79_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9780873386586-pp79-1">[1]</a></sup> Most names are of Islamic roots (e.g. Muhammed, Hassan, Ali, Ibrahim) whilst others are cultural (e.g. Guleed, Ashkir).<sup id="cite_ref-9783447036115-pp282_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9783447036115-pp282-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability"><span title="The material near this tag failed verification of its source citation(s). (December 2018)">failed verification</span></a></i>]</sup> </p>
