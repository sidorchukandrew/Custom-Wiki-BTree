      <p><b>ISO 10218</b> <i>Robots and robotic devices — Safety requirements for industrial robots</i> is an <a href="/wiki/International_standard" title="International standard">international standard</a> for <a href="/wiki/Robot" title="Robot">robot</a> safety, developed by ISO/TC 184/SC 2 "Robots and robotic devices"<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> in parallel with the <a href="/wiki/European_Committee_for_Standardization" title="European Committee for Standardization">European Committee for Standardization</a>. It consists of two parts: </p> 
      <ul>
       <li>Part 1: Robots<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></li> 
       <li>Part 2: Robot systems and integration<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></li>
      </ul> 
      <p>As a European standard it has the designation EN ISO 10218-1 and -2 (not to be mixed with EN 10218). </p>
      <p>To address the new field of safety requirements of <a href="/wiki/Cobot" title="Cobot">Cobots</a> ISO/TS 15066 <sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> has been published in addition to ISO 10218. </p>
