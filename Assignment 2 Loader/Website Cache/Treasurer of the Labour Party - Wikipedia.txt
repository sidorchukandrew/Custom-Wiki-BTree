   <h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Treasurer of the Labour Party</h1> 
   <div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content"> 
    <div id="siteSub" class="noprint">
     From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia
    </div> 
    <div id="contentSub"></div> 
    <div id="jump-to-nav"></div> 
    <a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a> 
    <a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a> 
    <div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr">
     <div class="mw-parser-output">
      <p>The <b>Treasurer of the Labour Party</b> is a position on the <a href="/wiki/National_Executive_Committee" class="mw-redirect" title="National Executive Committee">National Executive Committee</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Labour_Party_(UK)" title="Labour Party (UK)">British Labour Party</a>. </p>
      <p>Although a post with little power, in the past, it was often hotly contested by people who later became big names in British politics: <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Greenwood" title="Arthur Greenwood">Arthur Greenwood</a> beat <a href="/wiki/Herbert_Morrison" title="Herbert Morrison">Herbert Morrison</a> in 1943, <a href="/wiki/Hugh_Gaitskell" title="Hugh Gaitskell">Hugh Gaitskell</a> beat <a href="/wiki/Aneurin_Bevan" title="Aneurin Bevan">Aneurin Bevan</a> in 1954, who in turn beat <a href="/wiki/George_Brown,_Baron_George-Brown" title="George Brown, Baron George-Brown">George Brown</a> in 1956, while <a href="/wiki/James_Callaghan" title="James Callaghan">James Callaghan</a> beat <a href="/wiki/Michael_Foot" title="Michael Foot">Michael Foot</a> in 1967. Since the 1990s, the post has typically been held by a senior member of one of the larger Trade Unions. </p>
      <p>Since the <a href="/wiki/Political_Parties,_Elections_and_Referendums_Act_2000" title="Political Parties, Elections and Referendums Act 2000">Political Parties, Elections and Referendums Act 2000</a> (PPERA) came into force, the Labour Party has had to register a Treasurer to the <a href="/wiki/Electoral_Commission_(United_Kingdom)" title="Electoral Commission (United Kingdom)">Electoral Commission</a>, who becomes legally responsible for various returns to the Electoral Commission. It has been the practice of the NEC to register the full-time <a href="/wiki/General_Secretary_of_the_Labour_Party" title="General Secretary of the Labour Party">General Secretary</a> as Treasurer under PPERA, rather than the elected volunteer Treasurer. This has created two Treasurer roles within the party, so to disambiguate these roles the elected Treasurer is often called <i>Party Treasurer</i>, and the PPERA Treasurer is often called <i>Registered Treasurer</i>. The party accounts are signed by both treasurers using these titles,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> though under PPERA only the General Secretary need sign them. </p>
      <p>In 2008 the post was contested by the incumbent <a href="/wiki/Jack_Dromey" title="Jack Dromey">Jack Dromey</a> and by human rights lawyer Mark McDonald, with Dromey being re-elected. In 2010, former Deputy Prime Minister <a href="/wiki/John_Prescott" title="John Prescott">John Prescott</a> was defeated by <a href="/wiki/Diana_Holland" title="Diana Holland">Diana Holland</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> </p>
