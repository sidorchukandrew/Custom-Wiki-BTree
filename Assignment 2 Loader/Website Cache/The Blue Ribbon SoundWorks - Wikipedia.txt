      <p><b>The Blue Ribbon SoundWorks</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Software" title="Software">software</a> company in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>. The company produced several <a href="/wiki/Digital_audio" title="Digital audio">digital audio</a> products for the <a href="/wiki/Amiga" title="Amiga">Amiga</a>, including Bars &amp; Pipes, a <a href="/wiki/Music_sequencer" title="Music sequencer">sequencer</a> described by <a href="/wiki/Sound_on_Sound" title="Sound on Sound">Sound on Sound</a> as "the ultimate in Amiga sequencing",<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> and SuperJAM!, a music composition tool.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Blue Ribbon also produced the One Stop Music Shop, a hardware <a href="/wiki/MIDI" title="MIDI">MIDI</a> interface and synthesizer based on the <a href="/wiki/E-mu_Proteus" title="E-mu Proteus">E-mu Proteus</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Other early products included Who! What! When! Where!, a <a href="/wiki/Personal_information_manager" title="Personal information manager">personal information manager</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> It was founded by Melissa Jordan Grey and Todor Fay, who went on to found <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.newblueinc.com">NewBlue</a>, a video technology company. </p>
      <p>Blue Ribbon was acquired by <a href="/wiki/Microsoft" title="Microsoft">Microsoft</a> in 1995, and Microsoft subsequently merged Blue Ribbon's technology with <a href="/wiki/DirectSound" title="DirectSound">DirectSound</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-acq_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-acq-5">[5]</a></sup> After the acquisition, Microsoft made Blue Ribbon's Amiga products available for free download on <a href="/wiki/CompuServe" title="CompuServe">CompuServe</a> while discontinuing official support.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> </p>
