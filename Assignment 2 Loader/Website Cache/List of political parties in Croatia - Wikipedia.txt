      <p>This article lists <b><a href="/wiki/Political_party" title="Political party">political parties</a> in <a href="/wiki/Croatia" title="Croatia">Croatia</a></b>. </p>
      <p>Croatia has a <a href="/wiki/Multi-party" class="mw-redirect" title="Multi-party">multi-party</a> system, with numerous parties in which no one party often has a chance of gaining power alone, and parties must collaborate in order to form <a href="/wiki/Coalition_government" title="Coalition government">coalition governments</a>. Between January 1990, when political parties were legalized in Croatia, and March 2015, 264 political parties were registered, out of which 118 have since been struck from the register.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> </p>

       <li><a href="/wiki/Alliance_of_Primorje-Gorski_Kotar" title="Alliance of Primorje-Gorski Kotar">Alliance of Primorje-Gorski Kotar</a> (<i>Primorsko-goranski savez</i> or PGS) – won 1 seat in 1992 (then called <a href="/wiki/Rijeka_Democratic_Union" class="mw-redirect" title="Rijeka Democratic Union">Rijeka Democratic Union</a> or RiDS) – won 1 seat in 1995; 2 seats in 2000; 1 seat in 2003</li>

       <li><a href="/wiki/Croatian_Civic_Party" title="Croatian Civic Party">Croatian Civic Party</a> (<i>Hrvatska građanska stranka</i> or HGS) – won 3 seats in 2011</li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/Croatian_Democratic_Peasant_Party" title="Croatian Democratic Peasant Party">Croatian Democratic Peasant Party</a> (<i>Hrvatska demokratska seljačka stranka</i> or HDSS) – won 1 seat in 2003</li> 
       <li>† <a href="/wiki/Croatian_Independent_Democrats" title="Croatian Independent Democrats">Croatian Independent Democrats</a> (<i>Hrvatski nezavisni demokrati</i> or HND) – active from 1994 to 2011; won 1 seat in 1995</li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/Croatian_Labourists_%E2%80%93_Labour_Party" title="Croatian Labourists – Labour Party">Croatian Labourists – Labour Party</a> (<i>Hrvatski laburisti - Stranka rada</i>) – won 6 seats in 2011; 3 seats in 2015</li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/Croatian_Party_of_Rights" title="Croatian Party of Rights">Croatian Party of Rights</a> (<i>Hrvatska stranka prava</i> or HSP) – won 5 seats in 1992; 4 seats in 1995 and 2000; 8 seats in 2003; 1 seat in 2007</li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/Croatian_Party_of_Rights_Dr._Ante_Star%C4%8Devi%C4%87" title="Croatian Party of Rights Dr. Ante Starčević">Croatian Party of Rights Dr. Ante Starčević</a> (<i>Hrvatska stranka prava dr. Ante Starčević</i> or HSP AS) – won 1 seat in 2011; 3 seats in 2015</li> 
       <li>† <a href="/wiki/Dalmatian_Action" title="Dalmatian Action">Dalmatian Action</a> (<i>Dalmatinska akcija</i> or DA) – active from 1990 to 2003; won 1 seat in 1992</li> 
       <li>† <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Centre_(Croatia)" title="Democratic Centre (Croatia)">Democratic Centre</a> (<i>Demokratski centar</i> or DC) – active from 2000 to 2016; won 1 seat in 2003</li> 
       <li>† <a href="/wiki/Liberal_Party_(Croatia)" title="Liberal Party (Croatia)">Liberal Party</a> (<i>Liberalna stranka</i> or LS) – active from 1998 to 2006; won 2 seats in 2000 and 2003</li> 
       <li>† <a href="/w/index.php?title=Party_of_Croatian_Intergenerational_Solidarity&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Party of Croatian Intergenerational Solidarity (page does not exist)">Party of Croatian Intergenerational Solidarity</a> (<i>Stranka međugeneracijske solidarnosti Hrvatske</i> or SMSH) – active from 2016 to 2017; had 1 seat in 2016 (merged into <a href="/wiki/Bandi%C4%87_Milan_365_-_Labour_and_Solidarity_Party" title="Bandić Milan 365 - Labour and Solidarity Party">Bandić Milan 365 - Labour and Solidarity Party</a> in November 2017)</li> 
       <li>† <a href="/wiki/Party_of_Liberal_Democrats" title="Party of Liberal Democrats">Party of Liberal Democrats</a> (<i>Stranka liberalnih demokrata</i> or LIBRA) – active from 2002 to 2005; won 3 seats in 2003</li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/Serb_People%27s_Party_(Croatia)" title="Serb People's Party (Croatia)">Serb People's Party</a> (<i>Srpska narodna stranka/Српска народна странка</i> or SNS/CHC) – won 3 seats in 1992; 2 seats in 1995; 1 seat in 2000</li> 
       <li>† <a href="/wiki/Slavonia-Baranja_Croatian_Party" title="Slavonia-Baranja Croatian Party">Slavonia-Baranja Croatian Party</a> (<i>Slavonsko-baranjska hrvatska stranka</i> or S-BHS) – active from 1992 to 2008, won 1 seat in 1995; 1 seat in 2000</li> 
       <li>† <a href="/wiki/Social_Democratic_Action_of_Croatia" title="Social Democratic Action of Croatia">Social Democratic Action of Croatia</a> (<i>Akcija socijaldemokrata Hrvatske</i> or ASH) – active between 1994 and 2016; won 1 seat in 1995</li>
      </ul>
